Sarah is clear: 170+ websites need updating in 60 days, and the answer is not Wyatt. Wyatt should move under Mike's reporting. Mike (as creative director, future VP) should own the website build/update problem. The AI enablement company will build tools to connect audit findings directly to site implementations. In 4 weeks, Wyatt should not be a bottleneck — AI tools handle the bulk. Charlie's 404 error spreadsheets sent to Wyatt with no prioritization is exactly the problem we're solving.

3. Yext White Glove Service for GBP — Leverage It

SGA just signed a Yext white glove deal at $20/location/month covering Google My Business + Yelp across all locations. The team should be updating all GBP profiles. Key question: can we run the GBP audit, hand findings to Yext, and have them optimize? If yes, this dramatically reduces the GBP remediation burden on OMs and Market Managers.

4. Nathan/Miles Need to Book the Handoff Meeting

The Modus GP practices are blocked on operations. Nathan (head of operations for these practices) and Miles (COO) need to book the initial handoff meeting. Sarah bumped the email chain on 3/18 — Miles' assistant provided times, Miles never responded. We are not going to go into practices without a consolidated plan. Sarah's position: "We're ready. Book the meeting."

5. Specialty Practices Moving Ahead with Matt Sesto

Matt Sesto is driving specialty practice integration. Jamie has a call scheduled for specialty tomorrow (3/25). Specialty track is on a separate, faster path. This confirms the perio performance reports and Referral Lab work should proceed independently of the Modus GP blocker.

6. Pilot Process — Don't Stress the First Few Reps

Sarah emphasized: these first few practices are the pilot. Everyone (Sarah, Amy, Mike) will be heavily involved. The goal is to use the AI tools on one practice, then three, then iterate. Don't try to change the world immediately. Build the process right so it scales.

Many Modus practices (non-full-arch) don't have a CRM system, so patient funnel metrics are unavailable. Dental Intel should be in all of them (need to confirm with operators). Getting practices onto a CRM is a prerequisite for meaningful performance reporting. This goes into the systems implementation conversation in Meeting 2.

8. Peak Report Review — Schedule with Mitch/Ops This Week

Amy to schedule a call with Sarah, Mitch, and Ops to review the Peak Family Dental production report together. Focus: how do we collaborate on data analysis? What do we agree/disagree on? How do we use this as the model for all future practice reviews? Sarah is open to joining this week.

9. Present to Operators Simply — 5 Foundational Things

When presenting digital storefront findings, keep it to: "Here are the 5 things we look at for a healthy foundation. Here's where you're weak. Here's the project plan to fix them. We'll come back in a month." Don't overwhelm with 50 audit items — surface the critical categories only.

10. Plugin/Agent Opportunity for GBP

Sarah flagged that GBP optimization is "not complicated work" and should become an automated plugin/agent that takes audit findings and executes the updates. This is a build opportunity for the AI enablement roadmap.

This plan outlines a 90-day integration roadmap to unify 260 dental practice locations under a consistent digital standard. Updated 3/24 to reflect Sarah Alofs' strategic direction: strategy before tactics, operator meetings before performance reports, and Mike + AI (not Wyatt) owning the website fix pipeline.

Two parallel tracks run simultaneously: Specialty practices (led by Matt Sesto, moving ahead now) and Modus GP practices (blocked until Nathan/Miles book the operator handoff meeting). Digital storefront audits proceed for both tracks; marketing performance reports only after operator discovery meetings.

When practices blame marketing, the real issue is often: they're not answering phones, case acceptance is 20% below benchmark, or they're chasing the wrong tactics. The 5-Star Experience program gives practices the data and framework to focus on what they can control — conversion, phone skills, treatment planning — instead of blaming marketing.
