Executive Summary

Peak Family Dentistry's organization-wide production declined 3.1% YoY ($2.178M to $2.110M), reflecting significant challenges in case dollar conversion despite strong new patient growth and improved appointment health metrics. The organization onboarded 113 more new patients (+39.5%), achieving 7.75 NPs per working day and generating strong demand signals. However, this growth is being partially offset by three critical headwinds: (1) case $ accepted % fell 6.4 points (46.2% to 39.8%)—representing a -$248K waterfall impact; (2) schedule utilization weakness at Flagstaff cost -$149K; and (3) appointment confirmation systems deteriorated across locations. The opportunity is clear: standardize case presentation and financial coordination, recover Flagstaff's scheduling capacity, and implement org-wide confirmation accountability. With these three tactical pivots, the organization has the patient volume and clinical momentum to achieve positive production growth.

Top 3 Org-Wide Tactical Priorities

1. Case Dollar Acceptance Recovery (URGENT)

Current State: Despite raising case acceptance rates from 75.4% to 78.8% (+3.4 pts), case $ accepted % plummeted from 46.2% to 39.8% (-6.4 pts). This -$248K waterfall impact is the single largest headwind. Patients are saying "yes" to treatment, but committing to smaller scope plans. Flagstaff is the exception at 57.4%, but Cottonwood (26.2%) and Sedona (35.9%) are materially underperforming.

Recommended Actions:

  • Standardized Treatment Plan Presentation: Develop org-wide treatment plan presentation templates and scripts. Ensure all clinicians present full recommended scope upfront, not pre-filtered cases.
  • Financial Coordination Training: Conduct quarterly training on payment plan architecture, insurance coordination, and out-of-pocket scenario planning. Front desk and clinical teams must speak the same financial language.
  • Case Acceptance Accountability: Track case $ accepted % as a KPI by clinician and location. Create peer-sharing calls where high-performing locations (Flagstaff) teach method to lower performers.
  • Flexible Payment Options: Audit current payment plan offerings. Expand interest-free financing terms, consider third-party lending partnerships (CareCredit), or in-house payment plans to remove financial barriers.
  • Impact if Solved: Recovering case $ accepted % from 39.8% to 42% (still below prior 46.2%) would add $88K in annual production. Full recovery to 46.2% would add $228K.

2. Schedule Utilization — Flagstaff Recovery (HIGH)

Current State: Flagstaff's VPWD collapsed from 43.4 to 37.4 (-13.9%, -$149K waterfall impact), the second-largest production drag. Despite strong case $ accepted % (57.4%) and solid new patient growth (+41%), Flagstaff is underutilizing provider capacity. Cottonwood proves capacity recovery is achievable (22.5 to 25.4, +12.9%).

Recommended Actions:

  • Capacity Audit: Analyze Flagstaff provider schedules weekly. Identify gaps (unscheduled slots, open chair time, reduced hours). Interview providers to understand barriers (patient volume shortfall, scheduling system issues, staffing constraints).
  • Benchmark Cottonwood Model: Cottonwood achieved +12.9% VPWD growth. Document scheduling practices, hygiene flow, and patient volume acquisition methods. Apply to Flagstaff.
  • Scheduling Optimization: Review appointment lengths and block schedules. Ensure buffer time is appropriate but not excessive. Test same-day scheduling protocols to capture walk-in and same-day opportunities.
  • Patient Reactivation: Flagstaff's unscheduled pool likely has recall opportunities. Launch targeted reactivation campaign for 6+ month overdue patients at this location.
  • Impact if Solved: Restoring Flagstaff VPWD to 40 (midpoint recovery) would generate approximately $75K in incremental production.

3. Appointment Confirmation System Overhaul (HIGH)

Current State: All three locations experienced confirmation rate decline (org-wide avg 56% to 45%, -11.0 pts). Digital confirmations fell 35.5% (3,295 to 2,125). Sedona is critical at 43.5%. Recaptured patients declined 14.6%. Low confirmation directly feeds no-shows, cancellations, and missed production—even though total appointment leakage improved, confirmation collapse is a leading indicator of future deterioration.

Recommended Actions:

  • Org-Wide Mandatory Confirmation Protocol: Implement standardized 48-hour confirmation via SMS + phone hybrid model. All front desk staff confirm appointments according to the same protocol. No exceptions.
  • Daily Confirmation Dashboard: Create daily reporting by location showing confirmed vs. unconfirmed appointments. Share dashboard at 9am huddles. Track confirmation rate by staff member for accountability.
  • System Audit: Verify digital confirmation (DI) system is functioning at all locations. Check for system downtime, data sync issues, or training gaps. Ensure staff know how to solicit confirmations.
  • Recapture & Recall Automation: Implement automated recall messaging for overdue hygiene patients. Assign a dedicated staff member at each location to manage phone recalls weekly and track conversion.
  • New Patient Follow-Up: For all new patients, secure confirmation of second appointment within 24 hours of first visit—before patient leaves the office. NP reappointment rate is strong (78%); lock in that commitment immediately.
  • Impact if Solved: Restoring confirmation rate to 55% (modest recovery toward prior 56%) would recapture approximately 500 confirmed appointments organization-wide, generating approximately $338K in incremental production (at $677 avg/NP visit).
