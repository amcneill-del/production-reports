Executive Summary

Peak Family Dentistry's organization-wide production declined 3.1% YoY ($2.178M to $2.110M), reflecting significant challenges in case dollar conversion despite strong new patient growth and improved appointment health metrics. The organization onboarded 113 more new patients (+39.5%), achieving 7.75 NPs per working day and generating strong demand signals. However, this growth is being partially offset by three critical headwinds: (1) case $ accepted % fell 6.4 points (46.2% to 39.8%)—representing a -$248K waterfall impact; (2) schedule utilization weakness at Flagstaff cost -$149K; and (3) appointment confirmation systems deteriorated across locations. The opportunity is clear: standardize case presentation and financial coordination, recover Flagstaff's scheduling capacity, and implement org-wide confirmation accountability. With these three tactical pivots, the organization has the patient volume and clinical momentum to achieve positive production growth. Across all three locations, the practice scorecard shows 18 passes and 16 fails on scoreable metrics, with consistent gaps in scheduling access (3rd Next Available NP Appointment at 23–46 days across all locations versus the ≤7 day benchmark), patient retention, and referral tracking. A bright spot: unscheduled opportunity dollars decreased across all three locations (4.9%–27.0% YOY), indicating positive patient recapture momentum.
