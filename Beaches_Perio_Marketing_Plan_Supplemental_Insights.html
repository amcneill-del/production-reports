The PRD Touchpoint Calculator we built established that the critical ratio to watch is NP per PRD Hour. Critical Finding — Touch Frequency Is 8x Below Target

At 5 touchpoints/week across 154 referring partners, Michelle is touching each referrer roughly once every 31 weeks — nearly 8 months between contacts. The PRD Calculator target is 2–4 weeks. Even at full capacity (10/week), she'd only reach each referrer every ~15 weeks. This math reveals a structural problem: 154 partners is too large a base for a single part-time PRD at any touchpoint cadence. The plan should explicitly tier the 154 into A/B/C segments and only target A-tier (top 30-40) for regular touchpoints.

Recommendation — Shift the In-Person / Virtual Mix

The plan defaults to 75% in-person / 25% virtual. The PRD Calculator analysis found that locations using 25% in-person / 75% virtual produced 3x the touchpoint volume without adding PRD hours. For Beaches, shifting to a 50/50 split while maintaining the same 20 hrs/week would increase throughput from 5 to approximately 7-8 touchpoints/week — a 40-60% gain with zero additional cost. Reserve in-person visits for A-tier referrers and lapsed re-engagement; use virtual (text, email, quick calls) for B/C-tier maintenance.

The Plan Covers Marketing — Add the Clinical Angle

The current plan correctly targets lapsed referrer re-engagement to move avg $ accepted toward $4,500. This is the right marketing lever. But the portfolio-wide YoY decline (47.0% → 38.4% Case $ Accepted) suggests there's also a case presentation and financial arrangement component that the marketing plan alone can't solve.

Recommended additions to the plan:

1
Audit the treatment presentation workflow Are comprehensive perio treatment plans being presented in full, or are providers breaking them into phases that lower the initial accepted amount? The $3,760 average could reflect phased presentation strategy rather than true patient decline.
2
Review financial options presented at consult If Beaches lacks a membership plan or third-party financing (CareCredit, Sunbit, etc.), uninsured patients referred for $8K+ implant cases may accept only the extraction and decline the implant. Adding financing can lift avg $ accepted without changing referral volume.
3
Track avg $ accepted by provider If Field's avg $ accepted is at benchmark but Velgis is pulling the number down (newer provider, possibly presenting less aggressive treatment plans), the fix is clinical mentorship, not marketing. Break this metric out by provider in the 30-day check-in.

Bottom Line

The existing Beaches marketing plan is well-constructed and correctly identifies the key levers. These supplemental insights don't change the strategy — they sharpen the execution by adding three dimensions the original plan doesn't cover:

1. Efficiency math: The PRD Calculator work reveals that touchpoint frequency at Beaches is structurally insufficient for 154 partners, regardless of whether the open req gets filled. Tiering + virtual shift are immediate multipliers.

2. Digital credibility: Velgis' thin directory presence is a silent drag on the ramp. Every lapsed visit and prospect visit is less effective if the GP's front desk Googles Velgis and finds nothing.

3. Portfolio context: The avg $ accepted gap is a portfolio-wide trend, not a Beaches-specific failure. Framing it correctly (clinical + marketing, not just marketing) builds credibility with practice leadership and avoids misplacing blame on the referral program alone.
