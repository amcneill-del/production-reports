Executive Summary

Dakota Dental and Wellness Center delivered a complex financial picture in Q1 2026, with production declining 3.6% (-$53,755) to $1.454M despite significant operational wins. The practice scorecard shows 12 of 19 metrics passing, with critical strengths in patient acquisition momentum, appointment leakage control, and referral tracking excellence. New patient volume grew 16.1% (168 → 195, +27 NPs), a standout achievement amid call center headwinds, and the team executed a transformational improvement in appointment reliability: no-shows plummeted from 8.7% to 0.6% (a 93% reduction), recovering approximately +$138K in production through improved appointment completion. The primary production headwinds were case dollar acceptance (dropped from 36.4% to 25.6%, -$270K impact) and visit productivity (VPWD fell from 41.33 to 36.73). Outstanding achievements include perfect referral tracking (0% missing — best in portfolio), excellent handoff ratio (0.92, above 0.85 benchmark), and 20.7% same-day treatment growth. The practice's core opportunity is reversing the case acceptance decline through payment plan expansion and case value optimization.

Operations / Scheduling

4. Address 24-Day NP Wait (Critical Access Barrier)

Owner: Office Manager / Doctor

Impact: Reduce call drop rate, improve 40% → 55%+ conversion

3rd next available NP appointment at 24 days is a massive access barrier and directly contributes to 40% call conversion rate (vs. 55% benchmark) and 36% missed call rate. Conduct scheduling audit: (1) Block analysis — are doctors protecting enough NP time? (2) Production analysis — are NP appointments front-loaded with high-value cases? (3) Hygiene handoff — are hygiene patients scheduling NP appointments at point of care? Target: reduce 3rd available to ≤ 14 days within 60 days. This directly unlocks call center metrics and NP growth.

5. 48-Hour Confirmation Protocol Enforcement (Sustain 0.6% No-Show Win)

Owner: Office Manager

Impact: Protect +$138K no-show recovery, sustain 0.6% rate

No-shows dropped from 8.7% to 0.6% (93% reduction) — this is the biggest operational win in the report. Ensure 48-hour confirmation protocol is locked in place. DI confirmations dropped to 53.5% — this may signal protocol slippage. Document confirmation process, train scheduling staff, monitor weekly. This metric is critical to protecting the appointment leakage improvement.

6. Pre-Appointment % → 60%+ (Currently 58%)

Pre-appointment confirmations at 58%, just 2 points below the 60% benchmark. Systematic approach: (1) Review pre-appt no-show/cancellation correlation, (2) Enhance digital reminder chain (email + SMS), (3) Train reception team on pre-appt communication. Very close to passing — should be achievable within 30 days.

7. Capacity Audit for VPWD Decline

Owner: Doctor / Office Manager

Impact: Potential +$50-100K production recovery if VPWD restored to 39-40

VPWD fell from 41.33 to 36.73 (-4.60 visits/day), costing -$145K in the waterfall. Investigate: (1) Schedule utilization — are chairs empty? Even a 2-3 visit/day recovery would unlock +$60-90K. This is a secondary but meaningful lever.
