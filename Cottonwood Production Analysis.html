Production Analysis: Q1 2025 vs Q1 2026 (Jan 1 - Mar 19)

Executive Summary

Cottonwood achieved production growth of 8.3% year-over-year, moving from $549,939 to $595,845 in gross production. This growth was driven primarily by significant new patient acquisition (+54% increase to 174 NPs) and improved appointment management. However, the data reveals a critical opportunity area: case acceptance percentage declined sharply from 42% to 26.2%, representing a $224K negative impact that partially offset gains from other areas.

The team demonstrated strong execution in appointment leakage control and schedule optimization, gaining an additional $96K in revenue through these improvements. Yet the case acceptance challenge suggests that despite bringing more patients through the door, there are specific opportunities to work with doctors on presentation, treatment planning, and patient communication that could unlock substantial additional production.

This analysis identifies three specific, actionable tactics focused on where the data shows the greatest opportunity for Cottonwood to build on its new patient success and recover the production that's currently being impacted by case acceptance dynamics.

Top 3 Tactics to Improve Production

The data points to three specific areas where focused effort can unlock additional production. These tactics build on Cottonwood's strengths and directly address the largest revenue headwinds identified in the waterfall analysis.

1 Recover Case Acceptance % Through Enhanced Treatment Presentation (Potential Impact: $56K–$112K annually)

The Opportunity: Case $ Accepted % dropped from 42% to 26.2% despite higher overall case acceptance (75.2%). This means more cases are being diagnosed, but patients are choosing to accept smaller portions of presented treatment. On $1.42M in cases presented, a 42% acceptance rate would yield $596K accepted; at 26.2%, you captured only $372K. Recovering just 5% of case acceptance would add $71K annually.

Recommended Actions:

  • Conduct a case presentation audit: Review how cases are being presented to patients. Are photos being used? Treatment sequences? Clear explanation of outcomes?
  • Work with hygienists on initial diagnosis communication: The drop in hygiene-diagnosed cases (9.6% → 5.4%) suggests opportunity to strengthen how treatment needs are first introduced.
  • Analyze "presented but not accepted" cases: Identify patterns in case type, dollar amount, or patient demographic that correlate with declining acceptance.
  • Schedule implementation meetings: Focus on consistency in presentation protocols across all providers and hygienists.
Estimated Impact: If case $ accepted % improves from 26.2% to 31.2%, that's +5% on $1.42M presented = +$71K in additional production. A 10% recovery (to 36.2%) adds ~$142K.
2 Strengthen NP Hygiene Re-Appointment Rates (Potential Impact: $18K–$36K annually)

The Opportunity: NP hygiene re-appointment rate declined from 35.4% to 30.5%—a loss of 4.9 percentage points. With 174 new patients, this represents approximately 8–10 fewer patients returning for their hygiene re-appointment. At $169 average per hygiene visit, each lost appointment is approximately $170 in direct production and diagnostic opportunity.

Recommended Actions:

  • Audit the NP onboarding and scheduling process: Review how patients are being scheduled for hygiene re-appointments at the end of their NP exam. Are they being scheduled in-office? Are barriers being addressed (price, schedule concerns)?
  • Strengthen same-day scheduling: Patients who book hygiene same-day have higher completion rates. Focus team training on offering clear appointment availability and value statements.
  • Follow up on declined scheduling: When NPs don't book hygiene, initiate a brief follow-up (call/text within 24 hrs) to address concerns and reschedule.
  • Benchmark against practice standard: Cottonwood's prior 35.4% was strong. Understanding what changed (staffing, communication, capacity) can help recovery.
Estimated Impact: Recovering 2.5 percentage points (from 30.5% to 33%) on 174 NPs = 4–5 additional hygiene appointments per quarter × $169 = ~$2,700 per quarter or $10,800 annually. Full recovery to 35.4% = ~$18,700 annually.
3 Rebuild Appointment Confirmation Rate & Recapture Protocol (Potential Impact: $22K–$44K annually)

The Opportunity: DI Appointments Confirmed % dropped from 50.9% to 46.4%, and recaptured patients fell from 34 to 27. While overall appointment leakage improved dramatically (broken appointments down 77%), this suggests that some scheduled patients are not being reliably confirmed, and fewer lost patients are being recovered. Each unconfirmed appointment represents diagnostic opportunity lost.

Recommended Actions:

  • Audit confirmation workflow: Review what changed in the confirmation process. Are reminders going out at optimal times? Are staff capturing complete contact information? Are confirm/cancel/reschedule responses being tracked?
  • Implement a recapture protocol: For patients who don't show/cancel, establish a structured follow-up (within 3–7 days). Train team on empathetic, value-focused rescheduling language.
  • Monitor confirmation metrics by hygienist and doctor: Identify which team members have strong confirmation rates and share best practices.
  • Review scheduling system: Ensure reminders are being sent via preferred patient channel (text, email, call) at times patients are most likely to engage.
Estimated Impact: Improving confirmation from 46.4% to 50% on 1,423 completed appointments ≈ 51 additional confirmed patients per quarter × $419 average production/visit = ~$21,369 per quarter or ~$85K annually. Recapturing 8 additional patients per quarter (vs. current 27/quarter) = 32/quarter × $419 = ~$13,400 annually.

Summary of Key Insights

Cottonwood is in a strong position with excellent new patient acquisition momentum and significantly improved operational discipline. The three tactics above are designed to work collaboratively—with clinical, administrative, and scheduling teams focused on specific, measurable areas:

  • Tactic 1 (Case Acceptance) is the largest lever, representing $56K–$112K in potential annual impact. This requires direct collaboration between clinical teams and doctors on treatment presentation and case acceptance tracking.
  • Tactic 2 (NP Hygiene Re-Appt) targets a 4.9-point decline and is directly controllable through enhanced patient communication and same-day scheduling efforts.
  • Tactic 3 (Confirmation & Recapture) leverages Cottonwood's demonstrated ability to manage appointment flow and adds rigor to patient follow-up protocols.

Combined, these three tactics have the potential to add $96K–$192K in annual production—equivalent to an additional 16–32% production growth. The execution lies in identifying root causes, implementing clear protocols, and measuring results weekly to ensure accountability and sustainability.
