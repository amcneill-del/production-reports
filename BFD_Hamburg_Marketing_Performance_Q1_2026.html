Executive Summary

Beaumont Hamburg faced significant headwinds in Q1 2026 despite volume growth, with production declining $70,656 (-7.5%) to $873,952 while new patient count increased by 11 patients (+4.9%) to 234 NPs. The team's scorecard shows 13 of 18 metrics passing, but three critical operational issues emerged: cancellations spiked from 0.7% to 6.2% (FAILS the 5% benchmark), appointment confirmation dropped from 62.1% to 49.4% (below the 50% threshold), and case dollar acceptance plummeted from 48.5% to 31.4% — the single largest production headwind. Positive highlights include dramatic improvement in hygiene re-appointment (from 90.1% to 95.4%) and reduction in unscheduled active patients (from 1,533 to 1,106, a 27.8% win). The practice's path forward centers on three imperatives: urgently address the 6.2% cancellation rate and 48-hour confirmation protocol, recover case acceptance through expanded payment options and treatment presentation coaching, and leverage the improvement in hygiene-to-patient stability to drive cross-over restorative visits. Patient attrition at 90.4% is critical and demands targeted reactivation campaigns.
