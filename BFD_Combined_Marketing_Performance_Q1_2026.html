Combined Marketing & Production Performance Report
Q1 2025 → Q1 2026

Executive Summary

Beaumont Family Dentistry's combined operations delivered mixed results in Q1 2026 compared to Q1 2025. Organization-wide production declined -$188,357 (-5.5%) to $3.24M gross production. New patient volume dropped -11.3% (-86 patients) to 678 total, driven almost entirely by BFD Main's -103 NP decline. However, Leestown is the only location with positive production growth (+7.3%, +$51.9K), suggesting that operational improvements at this location warrant replication across the organization.

On a positive note, hygiene re-appointment rates remain excellent across all locations (95%+), same-day treatment volume is stable, and appointment throughput remains consistent (9,785 appointments completed, only -40 YoY). However, the organization faces critical systemic challenges: Case Dollar Acceptance is extremely low across all locations (38.2%, 31.4%, 32.5%), attrition rates are dangerously high (Main at 118.9%, Hamburg at 90.4%, Leestown at 70.0%), handoff ratios are well below optimal (0.43-0.57), and call center performance (40% conversion, 30% missed calls) continues to leak new patient opportunities. Yelp presence remains weak across the board. The combination of declining new patient acquisition, extremely low case acceptance, and high attrition suggests the organization is losing momentum in patient flow and treatment acceptance — core drivers of production.

Key Action Priorities: Implement organization-wide case acceptance and financial presentation training, address systemic call center performance gaps, standardize hygiene-to-restorative handoff protocols, expand Leestown's online scheduling model, and launch a retention initiative to combat attrition. The scorecard data shows 13-14 metrics passing at each location, but the failures are in high-leverage areas (patient acquisition, case acceptance, attrition).

Organization-Wide Action Plan by Team

Clinical Team

Case Acceptance Training Standardization (Org-Wide Priority #1)

Owner: Doctor + Clinical Director

Implement standardized clinical presentation protocols across all three locations. Current case $ acceptance is critically low (34% org average, vs. 60%+ benchmark). Root causes may include: (1) insufficient comprehensive treatment planning in exams, (2) weak patient education on treatment necessity, (3) inconsistent chairside presentation by clinical team members, (4) lack of visual aids/intraoral cameras. Immediate action: audit current presentation scripts/protocols at each location, identify best-performing clinician(s), standardize their approach across all offices, implement case acceptance dashboard tracking by provider, and schedule monthly clinical huddles to discuss complex cases and acceptance barriers. Expected impact: $300-400K annual revenue recovery if acceptance climbs to 50%+.

Estimated Impact: +$300-400K annual production if case acceptance recovers to 50%

Hygiene-to-Restorative Handoff Protocol (Systemic Issue)

Owner: Hygiene Director + Doctor

All three locations have critically low handoff ratios (0.43-0.57 vs. 0.70+ benchmark). Implement a standardized handoff system: (1) DI presentation must happen on 70%+ of hygiene patients (currently only 51%), (2) create a formal handoff checklist (patient educated, case explained, radiographs reviewed with patient, restorative appointment scheduled before leaving), (3) training all hygienists on motivational interviewing and case presentation, (4) implement a handoff efficiency dashboard. DI confirmation is the gating factor — if hygienists find problems, they must present them to the patient with visual support. Expected impact: increasing handoff ratio from 0.50 to 0.65+ could drive $150-200K additional production from better scheduling of restorative work.

Estimated Impact: +$150-200K annual production through improved schedule utilization

Same-Day Treatment Expansion in Hygiene

Owner: Doctor + Hygiene Director

Same-day hygiene revenue collapsed 82% (-$116K), while restorative same-day dropped 6%. The issue is not throughput (hygiene appointments actually increased +204), but reduced diagnostics and treatment acceptance. Action: review the 51% DI confirmation rate — every hygiene patient should have a clear diagnostic assessment, with appropriate same-day or booked treatment. Audit why hygienists are not presenting diagnostic findings, implement a diagnostic workflow (sealed ultrasonic scaling, SRP diagnosis, periodontal case detection), and empower hygienists to schedule same-day treatment or book restorative cases. Combined with improved handoff protocols, this can recover the $116K same-day hygiene loss and increase case acceptance.

Estimated Impact: +$100-150K annual same-day treatment recovery

Attrition & Retention Initiative (Critical at Main)

Owner: Office Manager + Front Desk

Main location is losing more patients than acquiring (118.9% attrition) — a patient loss spiral. Hamburg at 90.4% and Leestown at 70% are both above healthy 30-35% benchmarks. Immediate actions: (1) conduct patient exit interviews with patients who last visited 12+ months ago (understanding why they left), (2) implement reactivation campaign targeting unscheduled active patients (3,967 org-wide), (3) review cancellation and no-show data for patterns, (4) strengthen recall system to ensure no patient falls through the cracks, (5) implement a loyalty/appreciation program for long-term patients. Unscheduled active patients represent $3.07M in future opportunity revenue — this should be a major focus. Expected impact: reducing attrition to 50% across the board could recover 50+ patients per quarter, representing $80-100K+ additional production.

Estimated Impact: +$80-120K annual production through improved retention

Marketing & Patient Acquisition

Address Call Center Performance Crisis (Systemic)

Owner: Office Manager + Scheduling Director

Organization is losing significant new patient opportunities due to call center performance: 40% conversion rate (should be 60%+) and 30% missed call rate (should be under 10%). These figures apply across all three locations. Actions: (1) audit current call answering scripts and train staff on solution-focused questioning, (2) implement call recording review process (monthly sample of 10-20 calls per location), (3) add a second line or implement virtual receptionist to reduce missed calls during busy periods, (4) track wait time metrics and conversion by time-of-day, (5) create a "best practices" standard from the highest-performing location/individual. This will directly impact new patient flow. Expected impact: improving conversion to 55%+ and reducing missed calls to under 15% could recover 40-60 new patients per quarter (+$150-200K annual).

Estimated Impact: +$150-200K annual production through improved call conversion

Yelp Strategic Initiative (Organization-Wide)

Owner: Marketing Director

Yelp performance is weak across all locations (only 37 reviews, ratings 2.4-3.3 vs. 4.9 on Google). While Google is strong, Yelp is a critical review platform for local search. Actions: (1) launch a targeted Yelp review request campaign (email, text, in-app prompts after appointments), (2) assign responsibility for responding to all Yelp reviews within 48 hours, (3) create a Yelp monitoring dashboard and set a goal of 100+ reviews per location within 12 months, (4) analyze negative Yelp reviews to identify service gaps, (5) consider a small budget for Yelp advertising to boost visibility. Expected impact: stronger Yelp presence will improve local search ranking and new patient inquiry volume, estimated +20-30 NP per quarter.

Estimated Impact: +20-30 new patient inquiries per quarter

Expand Online Scheduling Model (Leestown Best Practice)

Owner: IT + Office Managers

Leestown has achieved 41.1% online scheduling adoption, while Main and Hamburg are at 0%. This represents a major accessibility improvement and reduced call center burden. Actions: (1) audit Leestown's online scheduling implementation (platform, promotion strategy, patient acceptance), (2) replicate the same platform and workflow at Main and Hamburg, (3) set aggressive adoption targets (aim for 30%+ online scheduling within 6 months), (4) promote online scheduling in all marketing channels and patient communications. This will also reduce call center load and improve new patient appointment capture. Expected impact: 20-30% online scheduling adoption could reduce missed calls by 15-20% and improve conversion by allowing extended booking hours.

Estimated Impact: Improved scheduling accessibility and call center efficiency

Analyze Main Location NP Decline (Critical)

Owner: Practice Owner + Marketing Director

Main location lost 103 new patients (-27.7%), accounting for nearly all of the organization's -86 NP decline. This is not a marketing problem alone — may reflect local competition, staff turnover, quality perception changes, or operational issues. Actions: (1) conduct exit interview analysis on Main patients who didn't return, (2) review Google/Yelp sentiment specifically for Main location, (3) audit Main's new patient onboarding process for gaps vs. Hamburg/Leestown, (4) assess staff morale and turnover at Main, (5) consider a localized marketing push (direct mail, community events) to rebuild brand perception. This is a critical deep-dive needed immediately.

Estimated Impact: Recovering 50+ lost new patients could add $150K+ annual production

Expected impact: tightening schedule utilization could recover $100-150K in annual production.

Estimated Impact: +$100-150K annual production through better schedule fill

Appointment Leakage Reduction (8% vs. 5% Benchmark)

Owner: Front Desk + Office Manager

Organization-wide combined leakage is 8.0% (patient scheduled but doesn't complete for any reason), vs. 5% benchmark. With 9,785 completed appointments, this represents ~490 leakage events. (2) implement pre-appointment confirmation calls 24 hours prior, (3) track and report leakage metrics daily, (4) assign accountability to office managers for leakage reduction, (5) review cancellation reasons and address operational barriers (waiting room comfort, provider behavior, cost concerns). Expected impact: reducing leakage to 5% could recover ~80-100 additional appointment completions, representing $50-75K production.

Estimated Impact: +$50-75K annual production through reduced leakage

Critical Findings & Strategic Recommendations

Three Major Red Flags

1. Case Dollar Acceptance at Crisis Level (34% vs. 60% Benchmark)

All three locations show dangerously low case acceptance (38.2% Main, 31.4% Hamburg, 32.5% Leestown). This is a systemic organizational issue, not location-specific. The organization is presenting treatment plans worth $500K+ per quarter but accepting only 34% of the dollars. Root causes likely include: (1) insufficient comprehensive treatment planning, (2) weak patient education on treatment urgency, (3) inconsistent clinical presentation, (4) financial barriers not being addressed, (5) lack of visual aids or case acceptance tools. Immediate action: Conduct a clinical presentation audit, identify best-performing clinician(s), standardize their approach, and implement monthly case acceptance tracking by provider. Recovering case acceptance to 50% would add $300-400K annual production — the single highest-leverage opportunity for the organization.
2. Attrition Destroying Patient Base (70-119% vs. 30-35% Benchmark)

BFD Main is in a patient loss spiral with 118.9% attrition — losing more patients than acquiring. Even Leestown's "best" 70% attrition is double healthy benchmarks. Combined, the organization lost net +25 patients (down from +64 prior year). Unscheduled active patients dropped 24% (-1,255), representing a $1.1M loss in future opportunity revenue. Root causes need investigation: are patients leaving due to clinical quality concerns, staff turnover, cost, or simply scheduling access issues? Immediate action: Implement patient exit interviews, conduct reactivation campaigns targeting the 3,967 unscheduled active patients ($3.07M opportunity), review cancellation reasons, and strengthen recall systems. Even recovering 20% of unscheduled patients could generate $240K+ revenue. This is destroying the patient base and must be reversed.
3. New Patient Acquisition Declining (226/mo vs. 250+ target)

Organization-wide new patient volume dropped 11.3% (-86 patients), almost entirely due to BFD Main's -103 NP collapse (-27.7%). BFD Main's new patient crisis is not reflected in appointment volume — suggesting the issue is not provider availability but patient flow/marketing/call center performance. Call center metrics apply across all locations: 40% conversion (vs. 55%+ benchmark) and 30% missed calls (vs. <10% benchmark) are organizational leakage points. Immediate action: Deep-dive into Main's NP decline (exit interview analysis, local competition review, staff/quality assessment), fix call center performance across all locations (script audit, training, second line/virtual receptionist), expand online scheduling adoption (Leestown's 41.1% model to Main/Hamburg), and implement systematic Yelp review strategy (currently only 37 reviews combined vs. Google's 2,585). Recovering 40-60 NPs/quarter could add $150-200K annual production.

Three Major Bright Spots

1. Hygiene Re-Appointment Rates Excellent (96.1% Average)
Across all three locations, hygiene re-appointment rates are 95%+, well above the 85% benchmark. This indicates that patients completing hygiene are returning for follow-up, and the hygiene program is stable. This is a foundation to build on — if handoff ratios and case acceptance can be improved, the existing hygiene base will drive additional restorative production.
2. Leestown Performance Model (Only Growth Location, Best Operational Metrics)
BFD Leestown is the only location with positive production growth (+7.3%, +$51.9K), highest new patient net growth (+68), best scorecard (14P vs. 13P), highest online scheduling adoption (41.1%), and healthiest attrition (70%). Leestown's operational and digital strategy should be audited and replicated at Main and Hamburg. Questions to answer: What's different about Leestown's clinical team, office management, marketing, or patient experience that's driving superior results?
3. Google Presence Strong Across All Locations (4.9 Rating, 2,585 Reviews Combined)
The organization has built excellent Google reputation across all three locations (4.9 stars). This is a major strength for local search visibility. The opportunity is to maintain this strength while building Yelp (currently weak) and leveraging the Google presence to drive scheduled appointments and online engagement.

Immediate Action Priorities (Next 30 Days)

  1. Clinical Presentation Audit: Conduct a clinical presentation audit at each location to identify gaps in case acceptance protocols. Observe 10-15 patient interactions at each location, identify best-performing clinician(s), and document their approach (how they present cases, use visual aids, discuss financial options, etc.). Standardize this approach across all locations through training and accountability.
  2. Call Center Performance Review: Implement call recording review process (20 recorded calls per location), analyze for conversion barriers, and develop standardized training. Deploy second line or virtual receptionist at Main and Hamburg to reduce missed calls. Track metrics weekly by location.
  3. Main Location Deep-Dive: Conduct exit interview analysis on 20-30 patients who didn't return to Main, review Google/Yelp sentiment specific to Main, audit patient onboarding process vs. Hamburg/Leestown, and assess staff morale/turnover. This will surface root causes of the -103 NP decline.
  4. Unscheduled Active Reactivation Campaign: Segment the 3,967 unscheduled active patients by last visit date and launch tiered outreach (email for 3-6 mo, phone for 6-12 mo, phone+mailer for 12+ mo). Even 20% reactivation generates $240K+ revenue.
  5. Leestown Best Practice Study: Document Leestown's operational differences: online scheduling process, front desk protocols, clinical workflows, marketing channels, and anything else that might explain superior performance. Create playbooks for Main/Hamburg replication.

Methodology & Report Disclaimer

Data Sources: This report is compiled from Dental Intel workflow data and individual location performance reports (BFD Main, BFD Hamburg, BFD Leestown) for Q1 2025 and Q1 2026. The analysis assumes data accuracy and completeness as of the report generation date. Combined totals are sums of individual location figures unless otherwise noted (averages are explicitly marked).

Benchmarks & Standards: Performance benchmarks used in this report (case acceptance 60%, attrition 30-35%, handoff ratio 0.70+, call conversion 55%+, DI confirmation 60%+, online scheduling 25%+, Yelp rating 4.0+) are industry standards for mid-to-large dental practices. These may be adjusted based on practice-specific goals or market conditions.

Waterfall Analysis: The production waterfall is derived by summing the individual impact drivers from each location's waterfall analysis. The "Other/Unattributed" bucket represents factors outside the primary drivers (case acceptance, new patient volume, same-day treatment, appointment leakage, schedule utilization) — which may include service line mix, production category reclassifications, or adjustments.

Scorecard Methodology: Each location scorecard evaluates 19 metrics across six categories (Patient Acquisition, Retention, Schedule Management, Online Reputation, Referral Tracking). Pass = metric meets or exceeds benchmark; Fail = metric below benchmark; N/A = benchmark not applicable or data unavailable. The "3rd Next Available NP Appt" metric at each location may result in N/A if benchmark is not consistently tracked.

Limitations & Caveats: (1) New patient acquisition metrics do not account for referral source attribution accuracy — missing referral data impacts analysis quality. (2) Production data is reported as "gross" (pre-insurance write-off) to show comprehensive case volume; adjustments may apply at write-off stage. (3) Case acceptance percentages are calculated from presented vs. accepted case dollars, but do not account for patient financial agreements (case splitting, payment plans) which may improve effective collection rates. (4) Attrition and net growth figures are based on active patient roster changes and may be influenced by patient database cleanup or definition changes. (5) Same-day treatment revenue includes chairside treatment, immediate services, and same-appointment closures; definition consistency across locations should be verified. (6) Benchmarks are industry standards and may not reflect practice-specific goals — thresholds should be reviewed with clinical and business leadership.
