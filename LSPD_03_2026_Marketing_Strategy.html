Pillar 1: Social Media Strategy

Current State

Little Smiles has active Facebook and Instagram accounts but posting cadence and engagement levels are unclear from available data. There is no YouTube channel, no TikTok presence, and no blog. All discoverable user-generated content lives on review platforms (Google, Yelp) — zero social UGC from parents was found in search indexes. The practice has 7 dentists and a warm brand voice, giving it excellent raw material for content creation.

Goal (90 Days)

Establish a consistent 4-5 post/week Instagram cadence and 3-4 post/week Facebook cadence with a defined content mix. Launch one video series. Generate at least 10 pieces of parent-created social UGC. Build a 60-day content pipeline.

Immediate (Week 1-2) — Quick Wins

1. Create 5 review quote cards from top Google/Yelp reviews
Why: Reviews are your richest content source right now. "Dr. Jenny has the best bedside manner" and "would recommend for anyone, especially if your child may have anxiety" are gold.
How: Open Canva → use Instagram Post template → brand colors + review text + stars → schedule one per week as "Review Spotlight" series.
2. Create branded hashtag set
Why: No branded hashtag tracking exists. This enables future UGC discovery.
How: Primary: #LittleSmilesCedarPark. Add to bio on all platforms. Use with every post alongside #CedarParkTX #PediatricDentist #KidsDentist #CedarParkMoms.

Short-Term (Month 1) — Foundation

3. Build and begin executing 60-day social calendar
Why: Consistent posting builds the "active business" signal that converts website visitors who check social proof.
How: Use content mix: 40% educational (brushing tips, first visit prep), 20% team/culture (meet the doctor features, staff spotlights), 20% UGC/reviews (quote cards, patient milestones), 20% promotional (NP offers, seasonal campaigns). Schedule via Later or Buffer.
4. Launch "No Cavity Club" and "Bravery Award" programs
Why: Parents share children's achievements. These create organic photo moments that generate UGC.
How: Design certificates in Canva with social handles + hashtag. Print for office. Create Instagram-worthy photo backdrop. Encourage parents to tag @littlesmilescedarpark.

Medium-Term (Month 2-3) — Growth

5. Launch YouTube channel with monthly video series
Why: Zero video content exists. YouTube videos rank in Google search and build trust for parents researching pediatric dentists.
How: Start with 3 cornerstone videos: "Your Child's First Visit to Little Smiles" (office tour), "Is Dental Sedation Safe for Kids?" (Dr. Jenny Q&A), "Brushing Tips for Toddlers" (educational). Film on iPhone, edit in CapCut. Post to YouTube + Instagram Reels.
Social Media KPIs: Posts/week (target: 8+ combined), Follower growth rate, Engagement rate per post, UGC pieces generated/month (target: 5+), Review quote cards shared (target: 4/month)

Goal (90 Days)

Move website audit score from 47% to 70%+. Launch 5 individual service pages. Install complete tracking stack. Create a new patient offer driving 5+ additional NPs/month through the website. Establish baseline content velocity (2 blog posts/month).

1. Create and feature a New Patient Offer
Why: The audit found zero new patient offers. Parents comparing pediatric dentists will choose the practice that makes the first visit easy. A specific dollar offer ("$99 New Patient Visit including Exam, X-Rays & Cleaning") converts browsers into booked appointments.

Pillar 3: Review Strategy

Current State

Little Smiles has an excellent review foundation — 361 Google reviews at 4.8★ and 122 Yelp reviews at 4.3★ — but velocity has stalled. Only 3 Google reviews came in over the last 30 days vs. the 10/month target. The 5-star percentage (79.8%) is below the 90% benchmark, suggesting some recent reviews are lower-rated. Yelp reviews haven't been updated since December 2025. There's also a sentiment score of 87 (strong), 87 Nextdoor faves, and presence on 6+ directories.

Goal (90 Days)

Reach 400+ Google reviews while maintaining 4.8★. Hit consistent 10+ new Google reviews per month. Improve 5-star percentage toward 85%. Respond to 100% of new reviews within 24 hours. Display reviews prominently on website and social channels.

Immediate (Week 1-2) — Quick Wins

1. Reactivate/launch automated review request system
Why: 3 reviews/month means the system is broken or nonexistent. With 1,502 completed appointments this quarter (~115/week), even a 5% response rate yields 5+ reviews/week.
How: Configure Swell (or BirdEye/Podium) to send automated text 2 hours after appointment → "Hi [Parent], thank you for bringing [Child] to Little Smiles today! If you had a great experience, would you share it on Google? [link]" → Follow up by email 24 hours later if no action.
2. Respond to all recent Google and Yelp reviews
Why: Review responses show prospective parents the practice cares. Google also rewards businesses that respond with better local rankings.
How: Assign one team member → respond to every review within 24 hours. Positive template: "Thank you, [Name]! We love seeing [Child] and are so glad they had a great visit. See you next time! — The Little Smiles Team." Negative template: Acknowledge concern → apologize for experience → invite to call office to resolve → keep it brief and professional.

Short-Term (Month 1) — Foundation

3. Train front desk on in-person review requests
Why: The most effective review ask happens face-to-face at checkout when the parent is happy. Automation supplements but doesn't replace the personal ask.
How: Script: "We're so glad [Child] did great today! If you have a moment, we'd really appreciate a Google review — it helps other parents find us." Hand parent a card with QR code linking directly to the Google review form. Track asks per day.
4. Investigate declining 5-star percentage
Why: 79.8% five-star is below the 90% target. This means ~73 reviews are 4 stars or below. Understanding the themes in lower reviews reveals operational improvement opportunities.
How: Read all 3-star and below reviews from the past 6 months. Categorize by theme (wait time, billing, communication, etc.). Share findings with the team. Address the top 2 complaint themes operationally.

Medium-Term (Month 2-3) — Growth

5. Launch Google Business Profile posting cadence
Why: GBP posts appear directly in search results and improve local pack visibility. Most dental practices ignore this channel.
How: Post 1-2x/week on GBP: NP offers, seasonal tips, team spotlights, review milestones ("We hit 400 reviews! Thank you Cedar Park!"). Every post includes a CTA button ("Book Online" or "Call Now").
Review KPIs: New Google reviews/month (target: 10+), Google star rating (maintain 4.8+), 5-star percentage (target: 85%+), Review response rate (target: 100% within 24hrs), Yelp review velocity, Swell/review tool send rate vs. completion rate
