The "Other" Problem

633 tasks (22%) are categorized as "Other" — the second-largest bucket. Keyword analysis reveals at least 8 hidden sub-categories: Campaigns/Ads (160), Reporting (44), Domain/Tech (42), Patient Comms (37), Listings/GMB (35), Doctor Changes (33), Logo/Branding (27), and Slide Decks (19). An AI tool needs structured intake to eliminate this catch-all.

Critical Questions for Building the AI Tool

Organized by workstream — what your team needs to answer before building.

1. Content & Brand Standards

  • Brand voice: Do all 40+ offices share one brand voice, or does each market/office have its own tone? How much variation is acceptable?
  • Template library: What existing templates exist for flyers, emails, and social posts? Are they in Canva, Adobe, or something else? How many need to be created?
  • Approval tiers: Which content types can be auto-published vs. which need market manager review vs. ops director sign-off?
  • Legal/compliance: What disclaimers or regulatory language must appear on dental promotions? Are there state-level variations?
  • Image/asset library: Where do office photos, headshots, and logos live today? Is there a centralized DAM (digital asset management) system?

2. Intake & Categorization

  • "Other" cleanup: 22% of requests are miscategorized as "Other." Should the AI tool enforce structured categories with smart routing, or keep a free-text option?
  • Required fields: Many tasks have sparse data (empty columns). What should be mandatory per request type to reduce back-and-forth?
  • Requestor training: Should the tool guide requestors through a wizard-style intake, or accept free-form descriptions and use AI to extract structure?
  • Priority logic: How should the tool prioritize? By office revenue? By request type? By market manager capacity?

3. Integration & Technical Architecture

  • CMS integration: Which CMS platforms do the offices use? Can the AI tool push website edits directly to staging?
  • Email platform: What ESP (Mailchimp, HubSpot, etc.) is used? Can the AI tool draft and schedule emails via API?
  • Social media tools: Is there a social management platform (Sprout, Hootsuite, etc.) the AI tool should connect to?
  • Listing platforms: Birdeye appears in the data — is it the primary listing management tool? Can API updates replace manual edits?
  • Print production: How are print materials produced today — Canva, InDesign, outsourced? What format does the AI need to output?
  • Project management: This data lives in SmartSheet today — should the AI tool replace it, or feed into it?

4. Scale & Rollout

  • Pilot offices: PCCD Los Altos (242 tasks) and AID Artistry (162 tasks) exercise the widest variety of request types — are they good pilot candidates?
  • Market manager capacity: Jenn Freemyer handles 53% of all tasks. Is the goal to reduce her load, or to enable more offices to self-serve?
  • Self-service vs. centralized: Should office managers be able to generate their own content directly, or should all requests still route through marketing?
  • Volume growth: As new offices are acquired, task volume will grow. Is the AI tool designed for 50 offices? 100? 200?

5. Quality & Governance

  • QA process: Who reviews AI-generated content before it goes live? Is there a single reviewer or per-market?
  • Feedback loop: How will users flag bad AI output? How will the system learn from corrections?
  • Version control: For website edits and print materials, how are previous versions tracked and rolled back?
  • Metrics: What defines success — faster turnaround time? Fewer blocked tasks? Higher office satisfaction scores?

Bottom Line

Based on the data, an AI content tool addressing just Bucket 1 (fully automatable) would cover ~1,200 tasks/year — roughly 42% of all requests. Adding Bucket 2 (AI-assisted) brings that to ~2,000 tasks/year or 70% coverage. The biggest single unlock is fixing the "Other" categorization problem and building structured intake, which makes everything downstream more effective.
