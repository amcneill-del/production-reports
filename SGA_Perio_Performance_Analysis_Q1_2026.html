SGA Periodontal Portfolio

Q1 2026 Performance Analysis

Executive Summary

The SGA periodontal portfolio contracted 6.6% in production and 6.9% in new patient volume in Q1 2026 compared to Q1 2025. While the portfolio declined from $6.64M to $6.20M in production and from 1,439 to 1,340 new patients, this decline masks significant variation across locations. Woodyard Periodontics demonstrated exceptional growth of 37.1% in production ($808K to $1.11M), establishing itself as the portfolio's strongest performer. However, this growth was offset by declines at other locations, particularly Stoner Dublin (5.6% decline) and Alpharetta, which saw case acceptance drop 16.3 percentage points despite a 29.4% increase in production. Van Scoyoc Sanford shows anomalous data (production dropped from $778K to $37.8K) requiring immediate investigation.

Case acceptance metrics reveal a critical discrepancy. While Q1 2026 case acceptance appears dramatically higher in DI data (averaging 67.7% at portfolio level), this reflects a methodological difference from referral program data. DI case acceptance typically measures case acceptance among patients who have already scheduled, whereas referral program metrics measure acceptance among all referrals received. Both metrics are valid for their respective contexts.

The referral program faces headwinds. Active practices in the Go Grow dataset show moderate referral volumes (averaging 100-140 referrals monthly) with case acceptance rates in the 55-75% range—below the portfolio's DI benchmarks. Time to consult averages 13-16 days, and scheduled consult percentages are healthy at 85%+. Call conversion data for practices using PeerLogic shows mixed results, with NP call conversion ranging from 37% to 79%.

Appointment scheduling and no-show management improved modestly. Portfolio cancellation rates declined from 5.2% to 6.7%, while no-show rates improved dramatically from 5.1% to 1.9%. This suggests improved scheduling discipline and patient engagement. New patient per working day metrics show increases at several locations, with Stoner Upper Arlington and Alpharetta leading.

Key opportunities lie in production stabilization, referral program growth, and data standardization. The portfolio should prioritize investigating Van Scoyoc Sanford's data anomaly, replicating Woodyard's growth formula across other locations, and aligning all measurement methodologies to enable consistent, comparative analysis.

10-Location Portfolio Composition

The portfolio comprises 10 distinct perio practices ranging from small (<$105K Q1 2026 production) to large (>$1.5M). The portfolio includes 3 Stoner Periodontic locations (Dublin, Upper Arlington, New Albany), 2 Van Scoyoc locations (Sanford, Main), and 5 single-owner locations (Alpharetta, Apache, Artistry, Parkway, Woodyard).

Data Quality Note: Van Scoyoc Sanford shows dramatic production decline from $778K (Q1 2025) to $37.8K (Q1 2026). This appears to represent either a new/ramping location, a data collection error, or a significant operational disruption. Recommend immediate investigation.

Referral Program Opportunities

Program Growth Potential: The referral program appears to be in early stages for portfolio practices. Average active referral sources per practice ranges from 10-30 doctors, with total referrals typically 80-140 per month. To reach maximum incentive tier (>141 referrals/month @ $5K/month), practices would need either 25-40% growth in referral volume or deeper penetration of existing referral base.

Case Acceptance Discrepancy: Referral program case acceptance (55-75%) is notably lower than DI case acceptance (67-97%). This is expected because referral program data measures acceptance among all referrals received, whereas DI data measures acceptance among scheduled consultations. The referral program should focus on: (1) increasing scheduled consult % (already at 85%+), and (2) improving case acceptance among scheduled patients through clinical presentation and financial coordination.

Time to Consult: Most practices achieve consults within 13-16 days, which is good. However, reducing this further to 10-12 days could improve conversion rates and patient perception.
