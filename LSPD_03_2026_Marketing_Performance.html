1. Executive Summary

Little Smiles Pediatric Dentistry shows a mixed Q1 picture: production declined 6.2% year-over-year (−$55,877), while new patient volume grew an impressive 45% (40 → 58 patients). The practice scorecard shows 9 of 19 metrics passing, with key gaps in Patient Acquisition (call conversion and volume), Patient Retention (reappointment rates), and Referral Tracking (94.8% of new patients missing a referral source).

The production waterfall reveals the primary headwind was a 15% decline in schedule utilization (VPWD dropped from 39.29 to 33.39), costing an estimated $166,774. However, the practice recovered $73,875 through dramatic improvements in appointment leakage — combined cancel/no-show rates dropped from 14.2% to 5.5%, a major operational win. The large "Other/Unattributed" residual of +$110,444 suggests fee schedule increases or payer mix improvements are partially offsetting the production decline.

The most actionable opportunities center on: improving phone conversion to capitalize on growing patient demand, rebuilding schedule density, closing the referral source tracking gap, and accelerating Google review velocity to sustain the practice's strong online reputation.
