2. Critical Issues

Issues that must be resolved immediately to prevent user errors and SEO harm.

2.1 Broken Eagle Mountain Phone Link

Critical
The Eagle Mountain phone link in the footer uses href="(8015281844" — this is malformed with an incomplete phone number and incorrect formatting. Users clicking "call" from the Eagle Mountain section will encounter an error instead of initiating a call.
Fix: Change the href attribute to href="tel:+13854691432" to match the correct Eagle Mountain phone number (385) 469-1432.

2.2 Duplicate Page Title on Contact Page

Critical
The Contact page title reads: "Contact Us | Pony Express Dental- Your Trusted Dentist... Your Trusted Dentist..." — the tagline "Your Trusted Dentist" is duplicated at the end. This appears unprofessional in search results and harms SEO ranking signals.
Fix: Update the page title to: <title>Contact Us | Pony Express Dental — Daybreak, Saratoga Springs, Eagle Mountain</title>

2.3 Blog Has No Visible Publication Dates

Critical
Blog posts at /blog/ show titles but no publication or last-updated dates. Google uses content freshness as a ranking signal, and undated content appears stale. Users cannot determine if information is current or outdated, affecting trust.
Fix: Add publication date and last-updated date to all blog posts. Display prominently near the title using schema markup: <meta itemprop="datePublished"> and <meta itemprop="dateModified">

3. High Priority Issues

Significant problems affecting user experience, conversions, and SEO performance.

3.1 Broken Navigation Links

High
Navigation items "About Us", "Dental Services", "Patient Info", and "Schedule Online" link to "#" (no destination). These appear to be dropdown menus without fallback landing pages. Users with assistive technology or with JavaScript disabled encounter dead links.
Fix: Create dedicated landing pages for each category or ensure dropdown menus have proper ARIA labels and fallback hrefs. Test navigation with keyboard-only and screen reader access.

3.2 Multiple Duplicate Contact Forms

High
Three to four duplicate contact forms detected on the contact page, many hidden in modal overlays. This creates confusion for users and can cause tracking issues, form submission duplication, and analytics complications.
Fix: Consolidate to a single contact form with a dropdown location selector. Remove hidden/duplicate forms. Test form submission tracking in Google Analytics.

3.3 Inconsistent Phone Number Formatting

High
Phone numbers use mixed formats across the site: "385-412-9175", "(385) 412-9175", "tel:%20385-412-9175" (with URL-encoded space). This inconsistency harms branding and can break click-to-call functionality on mobile devices.
Fix: Standardize all display formats to (385) XXX-XXXX and all tel: hrefs to tel:+1385XXXXXXX format. Use a global find-and-replace across all pages.

3.4 Limited Service Page Content

High
Service pages (General Dentistry, Cosmetic Dentistry, Orthodontics, etc.) contain thin content — typically under 500 words. Minimal procedure descriptions, no FAQs, no patient testimonials, and weak keyword targeting reduce search visibility and patient education.
Fix: Expand each service page to 800+ words including: detailed procedure descriptions, what to expect during treatment, recovery time, cost considerations, FAQs, 2-3 patient testimonials, before/after galleries, and clear CTAs.

3.5 No Social Proof on Homepage

High
Despite 2,308 Google reviews with 4.62-4.88 star ratings across locations, there is NO Google review widget or rating badge visible on the homepage. Patient testimonials appear in a small slider without star ratings or Google attribution, missing a major trust conversion driver.
Fix: Add official Google Review Badge to homepage (top right or near CTA). Embed 3-5 featured reviews with star ratings, reviewer names, and Google attribution. Replace generic slider with Google-attributed testimonials.

4. Medium Priority Issues

Important improvements needed for SEO and user experience optimization.

4.1 Schema Markup Insufficient

Medium
Only 2 schema blocks detected on the homepage. Missing critical schema types: LocalBusiness schema for each location, AggregateRating for reviews, Service schema for treatments, and comprehensive Organization schema with multiple locations.
Fix: Implement comprehensive structured data: LocalBusiness schema for all 3 locations (with hours, services, phone), AggregateRating schema with review count and rating, Service schema for each treatment type, and FAQSchema for FAQ sections.

4.2 Heading Hierarchy Issues

Medium
Homepage contains 24 H2 tags but limited H3/H4 nesting. The flat heading structure does not properly organize content hierarchy, which harms content organization signals for SEO and makes page structure unclear for assistive technologies.
Fix: Restructure headings to create proper H1 > H2 > H3 hierarchy throughout. Each H2 should contain 2-3 H3 subheadings. Remove unnecessary H2 tags and use proper semantic nesting.

4.3 Missing Trust Signals

Medium
No credentials, board certifications, or professional association logos are displayed on the site. Team page lists dentist/hygienist names but lacks credential details. Missing ADA accreditation, state dental board licenses, or professional memberships undermines patient trust.
Fix: Display credentials prominently: add ADA and state association logos to footer, include dentist board certifications and years of experience on Team page, list professional memberships, and include trust badges (HIPAA compliant, patient privacy certified, etc.).

4.4 No Breadcrumb Navigation

Medium
Deep pages (like individual blog posts or service pages) have no breadcrumb trail. This harms user navigation and misses SEO opportunity (breadcrumb schema improves search visibility and click-through rate).
Fix: Add breadcrumb navigation to all pages deeper than homepage. Implement BreadcrumbList schema markup. Example: Home > Services > Cosmetic Dentistry > Dental Veneers

4.5 Insurance & Financing Visibility

Medium
Financing page exists but is buried in navigation. No mention of accepted insurance plans on service pages or contact pages, missing the chance to reassure patients about costs early in the decision process.
Fix: Add accepted insurance list to footer or sidebar. Link prominently from service pages with messaging like "We Accept [Insurance Carriers]". Add financing/payment plan information to service pages and CTA sections.

4.6 No Google Tag Manager

Medium
Google Analytics is present but GTM is not detected. This makes tracking implementation and changes harder without direct code modifications, slowing conversion optimization testing.
Fix: Implement Google Tag Manager (GTM). Migrate existing GA tracking to GTM. Add event tracking for form submissions, CTA clicks, and appointment bookings. Use GTM for A/B testing conversion flows.
