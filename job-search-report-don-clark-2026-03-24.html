Mention specific processes: content approval workflows, brand consistency checks, compliance audits, and how you managed competing priorities across 100+ stakeholders.
2. "Our CMS is Sitecore, not WordPress. How would you approach working with a new CMS platform?"
Talking points: Acknowledge the difference, then pivot to transferable skills. Emphasize that your role at Gen4 was about web operations management, not just WordPress administration. The core competencies — content governance, site performance, agile delivery, SEO, compliance — are CMS-agnostic. Mention that you've worked across multiple CMS platforms (WordPress, Webflow, HubSpot) which demonstrates adaptability. Express eagerness to learn Sitecore and note that your understanding of CMS architecture and content management principles will accelerate the learning curve.
3. "Describe a time you had to manage competing priorities from multiple stakeholders across different departments."
Talking points: Talk about managing website requests from 100+ practices, each with their own marketing needs, while maintaining standards and hitting sprint deadlines. Describe your prioritization framework — how you balanced urgent campaign launches with ongoing maintenance and long-term SEO optimization. Mention collaborating with the marketing team to align website changes with campaign calendars. Highlight a specific example where you had to say "no" or "not yet" to a stakeholder request and how you handled it.
4. "How do you ensure site uptime and performance at enterprise scale?"
Talking points: Walk through your internal server infrastructure project at Gen4 — you didn't just manage existing hosting, you built a new solution from scratch. Discuss monitoring practices, performance benchmarks, Core Web Vitals optimization, and incident response. Mention the 67% cost reduction alongside improved performance — showing you can balance budget consciousness with quality. If you use specific monitoring tools (Pingdom, New Relic, Google PageSpeed Insights), name them.
5. "Where do you see yourself in 3-5 years? How does this role fit into your career trajectory?"
Talking points: Express genuine interest in growing within a large healthcare system. Mention that you're pursuing a Bachelor's in Computer Science, which signals continuous learning. Position this role as a step from managing web operations for a growing DSO to managing them for one of the largest healthcare systems in Texas. In 3-5 years, you could see yourself leading a larger digital team, expanding into Director of Digital Operations, or taking on broader digital transformation initiatives across the organization.
