Interview Preparation — Included Health

5 Likely Interview Questions & Talking Points

1. "Tell us about your experience building web governance models for multi-stakeholder organizations."
Talking points: Walk through the Gen4 story — when you arrived, 100+ practices had no unified web governance. You built: content ownership policies defining who could request what, brand standards documentation, approval workflows (who signs off on content changes), compliance checklists for HIPAA/ADA, and quality standards for all sites. Describe how you balanced stakeholder autonomy with brand consistency. Emphasize that you created this from scratch, not inherited it. Quantify: "100+ sites, coordinating with marketing team and regional stakeholders."
2. "How would you approach modernizing our web platform and technology stack?"
Talking points: Reference your internal server migration at Gen4 — you identified a scaling problem (hosting costs growing linearly), designed a solution (internal infrastructure), executed the migration, and delivered measurable results (67% cost reduction + performance improvement). Walk through your methodology: audit current state, identify pain points, build a phased roadmap, execute iteratively, measure results. Show that you think about platform decisions in terms of business impact, not just technology preferences.
3. "How do you approach technical SEO at enterprise scale?"
Talking points: Describe your multi-phased SEO optimization plan at Gen4. Explain your methodology: site-by-site audits using Google Search Console and Google Analytics, identifying common issues across the portfolio (site architecture, Core Web Vitals, meta data, content gaps), then creating standardized fixes that could be applied at scale. Mention tools you use: Google Search Console, Google Analytics, Google Tag Manager. Acknowledge the AI-driven search (AIO) aspect — express enthusiasm for learning this emerging area.
4. "This role requires building systems that democratize web publishing. Tell us about a time you empowered non-technical teams to manage web content."
Talking points: Describe how at Gen4 you had to enable marketing teams and practice-level stakeholders to request and receive content updates efficiently. Talk about the systems you built: ticket management, templates, standardized request forms, documentation, and training. Emphasize that your goal was to remove yourself as a bottleneck while maintaining quality — that's exactly what "democratizing publishing" means.
5. "How do you ensure compliance (HIPAA, ADA/WCAG) at scale without slowing down the team?"
Talking points: This is Don's secret weapon. Explain that at Gen4, HIPAA and ADA compliance weren't afterthoughts — they were built into the governance framework from the start. Describe your approach: compliance checklists integrated into the content workflow, automated accessibility testing where possible, regular audits, and training for stakeholders. The key message: compliance doesn't slow you down when it's baked into the process.
