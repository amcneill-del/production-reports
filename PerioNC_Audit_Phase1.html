Digital Storefront Audit — Phase 1

Practice: Van Scoyoc Periodontics & Implants

Website: https://perionc.com/

Audit Date: April 3, 2026

Audited By: Gen4 Dental Marketing Framework (Claude)

Executive Summary

Van Scoyoc Periodontics & Implants maintains a solid digital foundation with strong trust signals, excellent Google and social reviews (4.8-4.9 stars), and a clean, professional design. The website successfully communicates the practice's two-location footprint and specialist credibility. However, there are meaningful gaps in conversion infrastructure (no new patient offer visible, contact forms are scattered in footer only) and SEO fundamentals (missing keyword-rich title tags, no dedicated location pages, no schema markup visible). The practice has room to optimize mobile experience, streamline CTAs above the fold, and implement more aggressive new patient acquisition signaling.

1.1 Conversion Strategy 50%

1.1.1 Primary CTA Above Fold Partial
Finding: The "Request Appointment" button appears in the hero section of the homepage with clear visibility. However, it's a text link that scrolls to the contact form footer, rather than a sticky top-bar CTA or direct booking link. The button itself is not highly prominent (text-based, not button-styled). The footer contact forms are the primary conversion mechanism.

Impact: Patients must scroll to access a contact form; no direct online booking above fold.
1.1.2 Click-to-Call Phone on Mobile Pass
Finding: The phone numbers are clickable links: (910) 692-6270 (Southern Pines) and (919) 890-9727 (Sanford). Both appear in the footer and are formatted as tel: links for mobile click-to-call functionality.

Impact: Mobile users can tap to call directly; improves accessibility for urgent cases.
1.1.3 Online Appointment Booking Pass
Finding: "Schedule Online" link in main navigation and footer points to https://app.neuralityhealth.ai/scheduler/c4d5b046-066a-4315-99f4-d208a1ec07d5. This is a third-party booking system (NeurailtyHealth) and is functional.

Impact: Direct online booking available; meets modern patient expectations.
1.1.4 Contact Form (≤5 Fields) Pass
Finding: Footer contact forms (two instances: Southern Pines and Sanford) have 5 visible fields: Name, Email, Phone, Message, and "Are You a New Patient?" dropdown. Clean, uncluttered design.

Impact: Low friction form design encourages completion.
1.1.5 New Patient Offer Visible Fail
Finding: No visible new patient offer, discount, or limited-time special on the homepage or footer. The "Are You a New Patient?" dropdown segments audiences but offers no incentive. No "New Patient Special" or "First Visit Free" messaging anywhere.

Impact: Missing key conversion lever; new patient acquisition not aggressively promoted.
1.1.6 Multiple Contact Methods Pass
Finding: Multiple contact paths available: click-to-call phone numbers (2), email link (info@perionc.com), contact forms (2), online scheduler, and Google Maps links. Excellent redundancy.

Impact: Patients have multiple entry points; reduces friction.

Quick Wins for Conversion

  • Add a sticky "Book Now" button to header that links to online scheduler or opens modal with new patient offer
  • Create a prominent "New Patient Special" badge or banner (e.g., "First Exam + X-Rays: $99" or "Free Consultation for Gum Disease") and place above fold on homepage
  • Replace "Request Appointment" text link with a high-contrast button (e.g., bright blue/green) that stands out against the hero background

1.3 SEO Fundamentals 37%

1.3.1 Unique Indexable Location Page Partial
Finding: Two office locations (Southern Pines and Sanford) are referenced in contact footer sections with unique addresses and phone numbers. However, there is no dedicated URL structure for each location (e.g., /locations/southern-pines/ or /sanford/). Location details are embedded in the footer contact sections of the shared homepage template. Google Maps links point to Google Business Profile listings (good), but location pages are not individually indexable.

Impact: Missing local SEO optimization; reduced visibility for location-specific searches (e.g., "Periodontist in Sanford, NC").
1.3.2 Title Tag (keyword + city + brand, <65 chars) Fail
Finding: Homepage title: "Periodontist Southern Pines NC | Van Scoyoc Periodontics & Implants" (63 chars, within limit). However, internal service pages likely have generic or weak titles. For example, the blog page title should be "Dental Blog | Periodontics & Implants in Southern Pines NC" but is likely auto-generated. Titles are inconsistent across sections.

Impact: Weak keyword targeting on secondary pages; lost SEO opportunity.
1.3.3 Meta Description (unique, <165 chars, with CTA) Fail
Finding: Meta descriptions are not visible in the browsable content or confirmed via page inspection. Likely missing or using WordPress default template descriptions without CTAs. A strong description would be: "Expert periodontal treatment & dental implants in Southern Pines & Sanford, NC. Board-certified Dr. Van Scoyoc. Book your consultation today."

Impact: Reduced click-through rates from search results; lost SERP real estate.
1.3.4 Single Keyword-Rich H1 Pass
Finding: Homepage H1: "Comprehensive Dental Care In Southern Pines & Sanford NC" (good keyword + location combo). H1 appears once on homepage in appropriate context. Service pages have dedicated H1s (e.g., "Dental Implants", "Periodontal Treatment"). Solid practice.

Impact: H1 structure supports on-page SEO; keyword relevance is clear.
1.3.5 Individual Service Pages Pass
Finding: Navigation shows dedicated pages for: Dental Implants, Periodontal Treatment, Gum Recession Treatment, plus sub-pages under Periodontal Treatment (Laser Gum Surgery, Scaling & Root Planing, Gingivectomy, Sedation Dentistry). At least 7 unique service URLs identified. Each appears to have unique content.

Impact: Good service page coverage; supports keyword targeting for procedure searches.
1.3.6 Local Schema Markup (JSON-LD) Fail
Finding: No visible schema markup (LocalBusiness, OrganizationSchema, or AggregateOffer) detected in page structure. Google Search Console verification is required but not visible. Schema would improve search visibility, rich snippets (star ratings, hours), and local pack eligibility.

Impact: Missing rich snippet opportunities; reduced Google Business Profile integration.
1.3.7 Google Search Console (verify manually) Fail
Finding: Cannot verify GSC setup or indexation status without account access. Practice should have verified perionc.com in GSC to monitor crawl errors, search performance, and keyword rankings. No indication of active GSC monitoring visible.

Impact: No data-driven SEO monitoring; blind spot on search performance.
1.3.8 Internal Linking Strategy Pass
Finding: Service pages link internally to related pages (e.g., "Dental Implants" page links to other services). Footer includes service links. Blog posts link back to relevant service pages. Navigation structure supports internal linking. No broken internal links detected during crawl of key pages.

Impact: Solid internal link structure distributes PageRank; helps crawlability.

Quick Wins for SEO

  • Create dedicated location pages for Sanford office with unique URLs, address, hours, and location-specific CTAs (e.g., /locations/sanford/)
  • Add LocalBusiness and Organization schema markup to homepage and location pages (use a plugin or hire SEO specialist)
  • Write unique meta descriptions for each page with CTAs (under 160 chars)
  • Set up Google Search Console, verify ownership, and monitor indexation and search performance monthly
  • Optimize internal linking: add "related articles" and "related services" sections to blog posts and service pages

1.7.1 Insurance Accepted List Fail
Finding: No insurance information, accepted insurance list, or insurance partners visible on the website. A patient considering treatment would have no way to know if their insurance is accepted without calling.

Impact: No financial incentive to book; competitors offering specials will capture price-sensitive new patients.

Finding: No mention of in-house membership program, savings club, or benefits plan for uninsured/underinsured patients (e.g., "VanScoyoc Perio Club: $10/month for 15% off all procedures"). These plans are common in specialty dental and drive loyalty and new patient acquisition.

Impact: Uninsured patients have no pathway to affordable care; lost long-term patient lifetime value.

Critical Actions for Offers & Financing

  • Create an "Insurance & Financing" page listing accepted insurance plans, partnered financing companies (e.g., CareCredit, Alphaeon), and in-house plan options
  • Design a prominent "New Patient Special" offer (e.g., "First Exam + Full Mouth X-Rays: $99" or "Free Gum Disease Consultation") and display on homepage banner and above fold
  • Consider launching an in-house membership plan targeting uninsured patients (e.g., "$15/month Perio Plus Club: 15% off all non-surgical treatment")

Scoring Methodology

Each checklist item is scored as follows:

  • Pass (1.0): Feature is present and well-executed; meets or exceeds standards
  • Partial (0.5): Feature is present but needs improvement; functionality exists but is suboptimal
  • Fail (0): Feature is missing or broken; does not meet minimum expectations
  • N/A: Feature is not applicable or cannot be verified; excluded from overall score calculation

Overall Score Calculation: Sum of all item scores ÷ total applicable items × 100

Grade Scale:

  • 80–100% = Strong (Green)
  • 60–79% = Good (Yellow)
  • 40–59% = Needs Work (Orange)
  • <40% = Critical (Red)
