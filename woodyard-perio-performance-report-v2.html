Executive Summary

Woodyard Periodontics delivered exceptional growth in Q1 2026, with production increasing 46.3% to $1.02M and new patient volume surging 69% to 127 referrals. The practice demonstrated remarkable operational improvements, including a dramatic 71% reduction in time-to-consult (from 47.6 to 13.9 days) and perfect referral source tracking (zero missing). However, the scorecard reveals critical gaps that are constraining growth: a 57.1% missed call rate is eroding the referral pipeline, schedule-to-consult conversion declined to 76.8%, and case value dropped 21.8% from $9,104 to $7,122. These findings point to operational excellence in some areas offset by bottlenecks in referral intake, patient follow-through, and case presentation. The practice scorecard shows 14 of 19 metrics passing, with key gaps in Patient Acquisition (missed calls, schedule-to-consult) and Case Acceptance (declining case value). The action plan focuses on three priorities: eliminating the missed call crisis, recovering schedule-to-consult conversion, and investigating case value decline by referring doctor.

Critical Findings & Opportunities

Perio-Specific Recommendation Groups

A. Referral Volume Gap Analysis

Finding: The practice is at 77 referrals/month (90-day) vs. target of 84/month. Gap = 7 referrals/month.

Financial Impact: 7 additional referrals/month × $7,122 avg case value × 12 months = $598K annual opportunity (equivalent to $50K/month).

Root Cause Analysis: With 69 active referring sources, the practice is averaging 1.8 referrals/month per source. Top performers likely refer 3-5/month while others refer <1/month. The 9 new sources added this year may be ramping up but not yet at peak volume.

Recommended Actions:

  • Query Referral Lab to segment referring doctors by volume tier (top 10, mid-tier 20, bottom 39). Identify which sources are below 1.5/month.
  • For the 9 new referring sources, track their referral volume monthly and provide early feedback/engagement if volume is slower than expected.
  • Target 3-5 additional new high-quality referring doctors in geographic areas adjacent to current referrer network.
  • Conduct quarterly business reviews (QBRs) with top 10 referrers showing patient outcome data and treatment acceptance rates.

B. Time-to-Consult Success — Sustain & Protect

Finding: Improvement from 47.6 days (2025) to 13.9 days (2026) is a 71% reduction and represents an operational transformation.

Financial Impact: Faster time-to-consult reduces referral leakage (GPs sending patients elsewhere if delays are long). This metric is now benchmark-leading.

Recommended Actions:

  • Document the operational changes that drove this improvement (scheduling, staffing, communication protocol).
  • Monitor weekly KPI dashboard showing average time-to-consult. Alert if it exceeds 14 days.
  • Share the improvement story with top 10 referring doctors to reinforce the value of sending referrals to Woodyard.

C. Case Value Erosion Deep-Dive

Finding: Average case value declined 21.8% from $9,104 (2025) to $7,122 (2026). This is a $1,982 per-case decline.

Potential Causes: (a) Referring doctors are sending less complex cases, (b) Patient selection of smaller treatment plans, (c) Insurance mix shift toward lower-benefit plans, (d) Treatment planning philosophy shift toward less-aggressive approaches.

Investigation Steps:

  • Query Referral Lab to segment case value by referring doctor. Identify which referrers' cases declined in value.
  • Audit 20-30 treatment plans from 2025 vs. 2026 to see if full-scope perio protocols are being presented consistently.
  • Analyze accepted case values to determine if patients are cherry-picking smaller procedures from presented plans.
  • Review insurance mix and benefits to see if payer changes are driving lower case acceptance rates.

D. Schedule-to-Consult Recovery Plan

Finding: 76.8% of referred patients who are scheduled actually attend consult (down from 80.4%). This means ~18 patients per quarter who were scheduled don't show.

Financial Impact: 18 patients × $7,122 avg case value = ~$128K per quarter in potential lost production.

Root Causes to Investigate:

  • Insurance verification before consult — do patients discover they have low/no perio benefits and cancel?
  • Consult scheduling wait times — are patients scheduling far in advance and then forgetting?
  • Patient communication — are appointment reminders being sent? Are referring doctors making warm handoff calls?

Recommended Actions:

  • Implement 48-hour pre-consult confirmation call (phone or SMS). Track confirmation rate daily.
  • Send welcome packet to referred patients before consult with practice info, parking, insurance forms.
  • Have referring doctor's office make a warm handoff call introducing the patient to Woodyard.
  • Verify insurance benefits before consult and pre-brief patient on coverage.

E. Missed Call Emergency Protocol

Finding: 57.1% missed call rate is CRITICAL. For a referral-based practice, when a GP's office calls to refer a patient and gets voicemail, they often send that patient to another specialist.

Financial Impact: Assuming 100-120 referral calls/month and 57% miss rate = 57-68 missed opportunities per month. At $7,122 avg case value, this represents $406K-$485K in annual missed production.

Recommended Actions:

  • Audit current phone coverage during business hours. Identify gaps (lunch hours, end of day, etc.).
  • Implement dedicated phone line with warm greeting and immediate triage to scheduling staff.
  • Use overflow answering service for calls that cannot be answered in-office within 2 minutes.
  • Track missed calls by time of day. Analyze patterns to determine if staffing needs adjustment.
  • Callback protocol: Return all missed referral calls within 2 hours with apology and immediate scheduling offer.
  • Weekly KPI: Track missed call rate and trend toward goal of <18%.

F. Unscheduled Patient Opportunity Pool

Finding: 353 unscheduled active patients with $1.17M in opportunity. 0-6 month overdue patients nearly doubled: 105→153 (+45.7%).

Action Plan:

  • Pull the 153 patients in the 0-6 month overdue bucket from DI Analytics (NOT Engagement, which excludes inactive patients).
  • Segment by last visit date. Prioritize patients 0-3 months overdue (highest conversion likelihood).
  • Assign manual phone outreach to front desk with script tailored to reason for lapse (maintenance, post-op follow-up, etc.).
  • Track conversion weekly. Target to recover 30% of the 153 patients = 45 patients = ~$320K in recovery.

G. Referring Doctor Communication Protocol

Finding: With 69 active sources (+9 new this year), the practice has a growing referral network that needs ongoing engagement.

Recommended Actions:

  • Quarterly progress reports to top 10 referring doctors showing: patient volume, case acceptance rates, successful outcomes, implant placement rates.
  • Personal thank-you calls from Dr. Woodyard to top 5 referrers (quarterly).
  • Invite top referrers to CE events, lunch & learns, or implant case reviews.
  • Ensure timely post-op summaries and treatment outcome reports are sent back to referring GPs within 1 week of treatment.

H. Case Acceptance Presentation Audit

Finding: DI shows 95.6% case acceptance (some treatment) but Referral Lab shows 60.8% (full perio plan). Gap of 34.8% means one-third of patients accept some treatment but reject the full recommended plan.

Recommended Actions:

  • Standardize full-scope presentation: Phase I (initial therapy) → Phase II (surgical) → Phase III (maintenance). Present all phases upfront.
  • Create visual treatment plan displays showing full pathway and total investment.
  • Train staff on overcoming common objections to surgical cases (fear, cost, complexity).
  • Track case acceptance by provider to identify differences in presentation technique.

I. Financial Counseling Enhancement

Finding: Case $ Accepted % dropped from 61% to 46.1%. Patients are accepting less of what's presented — likely a financial barrier.

Recommended Actions:

  • Present CareCredit and in-house financing options BEFORE treatment plan presentation, not after.
  • Offer phased payment plans: e.g., pay 25% at Phase I, 50% at Phase II, 25% at Phase III.
  • Verify insurance benefits before consult so financial counseling is accurate and patient doesn't discover denied benefits later.
  • Have financial coordinator available during consult to answer questions and close objections.

J. Maintenance Compliance Protection

Finding: Overall hygiene re-appointment rate is excellent (95.2%), but need to ensure surgical patients transition to maintenance efficiently.

Recommended Actions:

  • Before patient leaves surgical visit, schedule their first maintenance appointment (3-4 month perio cycle).
  • Brief patient on the maintenance schedule and importance of compliance.
  • Set up automated recall TouchPoint in DI Engagement for ACTIVE maintenance patients (3-4 month triggers).
  • Manual phone recall for patients who don't respond to automated reminders.
