Executive Summary

Sedona's production declined 5.2% year-over-year ($469,020 to $444,726), despite meaningful clinical and patient volume gains. New patient volume increased 18% (83 to 98), case acceptance % improved 6.5 points (70.9% to 77.4%), and NP case acceptance rose to 88.1%. However, three critical headwinds offset these wins: (1) Case acceptance dollars dropped 4.9 points despite higher case %, (2) appointment leakage worsened significantly (6.5% to 8.2%), and (3) appointment confirmations plummeted (57.7% to 43.5%). The opportunity lies in stabilizing appointment management and recapturing lost case dollars through improved follow-up and patient acceptance processes.

Top 3 Tactical Opportunities

1. Reduce Appointment Leakage (Priority: Urgent)

Current State: Broken appointments surged 73 to 98 (34% increase), cancellations up 62% (53 to 86), and overall leakage worsened from 6.5% to 8.2%. Sedona is the only location where leakage deteriorated.

Recommended Actions:

  • Root Cause Analysis: Conduct exit interviews with patients who cancel/no-show. Is it scheduling conflicts, transportation, cost concerns, or lack of reminder effectiveness?
  • Confirmation Protocol: Given DI confirmations fell 40%, implement mandatory SMS/phone confirmations 48 hours pre-appointment. Track confirmation rate daily.
  • Cancellation Policy Review: Evaluate whether cancellation policies are being enforced. Consider incentives for 48-hour cancellation notice.
  • Hygiene Scheduling Pressure: Hygiene visits/hour doubled (2.77 to 5.58), suggesting overbooking. Normalize appointment density to reduce burnout and patient dissatisfaction.
  • Impact if Solved: Reducing leakage from 8.2% to 6.5% (prior level) would recover approximately $7.6K in annual production.

2. Improve Case $ Accepted % (Priority: High)

Current State: Despite raising case acceptance % from 70.9% to 77.4%, the dollar value of accepted cases dropped 4.9 points (40.8% to 35.9%). Patients are saying "yes" to smaller treatment plans.

Recommended Actions:

  • Treatment Plan Presentation Audit: Review case presentation consistency. Are clinicians presenting full recommended scope, or reducing scope before presentation?
  • Financial Arrangements: Analyze whether payment plan availability, insurance coverage, or out-of-pocket costs are driving lower case acceptance dollars. Consider flexible payment options.
  • Chairside Communication Training: Reinforce clinical rationale for recommended treatment. Patients who understand the "why" are more likely to accept larger plans.
  • Case Staging Strategy: For large cases, develop a systematic approach to phase treatment (Phase 1: urgent, Phase 2: restorative, Phase 3: cosmetic). Document planned phases at initial presentation.
  • Impact if Solved: Recovering even 2 points on case $ accepted % would add approximately $15.6K in annual production.

3. Rebuild Appointment Confirmation & Recapture Systems (Priority: High)

Current State: DI confirmed appointments fell 40% (795 to 478), confirmation rate dropped 14.2 points to 43.5%, and recaptured patients declined 33% (18 to 12). This is the weakest confirmation rate among all three locations.

Recommended Actions:

  • DI Confirmation Audit: Verify that the DI system is functioning and that staff are actively soliciting confirmations during final checkout. Check for system downtime or data synchronization issues.
  • Reactivation Campaign: Target the 622 unscheduled active patients and 183 patients lost from the pool with a focused reactivation program (phone calls, emails, or mailings).
  • Recall & Recare Protocol: Implement automated recall messages for patients overdue for hygiene (beyond 6 months). Assign a team member to manage phone recalls weekly.
  • New Patient Follow-Up Timing: For new patients, confirm second appointment within 24 hours of first visit before they leave the office. NP hygiene re-appointment rate is strong at 49%; ensure all re-appointments are confirmed.
  • Impact if Solved: Restoring confirmation rate to 50% (baseline) would recapture approximately 80 confirmed appointments, generating approximately $33K in marginal production.
