Executive Summary

Sedona's production declined 5.2% year-over-year ($469,020 to $444,726), despite meaningful clinical and patient volume gains. New patient volume increased 18% (83 to 98), case acceptance % improved 6.5 points (70.9% to 77.4%), and NP case acceptance rose to 88.1%. However, three critical headwinds offset these wins: (1) Case acceptance dollars dropped 4.9 points despite higher case %, (2) appointment leakage worsened significantly (6.5% to 8.2%), and (3) appointment confirmations plummeted (57.7% to 43.5%). The opportunity lies in stabilizing appointment management and recapturing lost case dollars through improved follow-up and patient acceptance processes. The practice scorecard shows 5 of 9 scoreable metrics passing, with key gaps in patient retention (unscheduled patients, NP re-appointment rate) and referral tracking.
