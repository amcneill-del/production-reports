Executive Summary

Beaumont Family Dentistry (Main) experienced a challenging Q1 2026 with production declining 9.6% to $1,604,828 from $1,774,380 year-over-year. This decline was driven primarily by a sharp 27.7% drop in new patient volume (372 → 269), combined with elevated patient attrition of 118.9% — meaning more patients are leaving the practice than joining it, with net growth at -72 patients. However, the practice achieved a critical data integrity win: referral source tracking improved dramatically from 100% missing (372/372) to 1.5% missing (4/269), providing clear visibility into patient acquisition channels. Scorecard results show 13 of 18 metrics passing, with primary gaps in new patient conversion infrastructure (call conversion 40% vs. 55% benchmark, missed calls 30% vs. 18% benchmark) and appointment leakage that worsened from 2.3% to 7.3% combined (no-shows: 1.9% → 4.3%, cancellations: 0.4% → 3.0%). Bright spots include a dramatic improvement in hygiene re-appointment (82.9% → 95.3%), NP hygiene re-appointment (41.9% → 59.9%), and unscheduled active patient reduction (2,766 → 2,036), signaling strong retention fundamentals once patients are engaged. The priority for recovery is reversing the new patient volume decline through marketing reactivation and addressing the alarming handoff ratio of 0.43 (well below the 0.85 benchmark), indicating that hygiene-to-restorative conversion is critically underperforming.
