Peninsula Center of Cosmetic Dentistry (PCCD) operates as a premium, fee-for-service practice targeting the top 10% patient base in the Bay Area. Dr. Joseph Field brings exceptional credentials (DABOI/ID Diplomate, USC faculty, 20+ years experience) and PCCD has established strong social proof (4.9 stars, 210 Yelp + 295 Birdeye reviews, 35K Instagram followers). Why This Matters

The practice has strong conversion infrastructure (service pages, reviews, gallery) but weak awareness-stage demand generation. Cosmetic case acceptance is high-consideration (patients research extensively, seek authority validation, fear overly-aggressive outcomes). A premium patient targeting the top 10% is unlikely to book based solely on practice reviews—they want to see the doctor's credentials, philosophy, case selection, and patient results at scale. The content engine shifts investment toward long-form authority, case storytelling, and doctor-led demand creation.

Brand Architecture: Doctor Brand vs. Practice Brand

Effective content marketing for premium cosmetic dentistry requires clear separation of two distinct brands with different strategic purposes, content types, and audience journeys.

Strategic Framework

Doctor Brand (@dr_joseph_field)

Primary Purpose: Demand Creation

Audience: High-intent patients researching cosmetic dentistry, industry peers, referral sources

Messaging: Authority, expertise, philosophy, education, credentials, thought leadership

Content Types: Educational videos, case commentary, speaking highlights, credentialing explanation, patient education, Q&A, industry insights

Tone: Authoritative, educational, confident, sophisticated

Platforms: YouTube (primary), Instagram (@dr_joseph_field), LinkedIn, blog on drjosephfield.com, Catapult Education content

Conversion Mechanism: "I trust this doctor's credentials and approach" → link to practice site for booking

Practice Brand (@peninsulacosmeticdentistry)

Primary Purpose: Demand Conversion

Audience: Qualified prospects ready to book, existing patients, local community, insurance/referral sources

Messaging: Social proof, convenience, outcomes, patient success stories, smile gallery, service options, financing

Content Types: Before/afters, patient testimonials, service pages, location info, financing options, gallery highlights, community events

Tone: Approachable, friendly, results-focused, community-oriented

Platforms: Instagram (primary), Facebook, TikTok, website, Google Business, Yelp

Conversion Mechanism: "I see results, patients are happy, I trust them to book" → booking link, consultation CTA

The Handoff: From Demand Creation to Demand Conversion

Doctor Brand → Practice Brand Funnel:

  1. Awareness: Patient discovers @dr_joseph_field YouTube video on "Porcelain Veneers: What to Expect" (educational content, DSD explanation, before/after commentary)
  2. Authority Validation: Patient sees Dr. Field's DABOI/ID credentials, USC faculty status, 20+ year experience, speaking history. Video links to biography on drjosephfield.com
  3. Practice Discovery: Patient clicks through to pccdsmiles.com, sees DSD Certified badge, patient gallery (86 Yelp photos), 4.9 star rating, location/hours
  4. Social Proof: Patient browses @peninsulacosmeticdentistry Instagram, reads patient testimonials, sees before/afters, verifies Yelp reviews (210 reviews, 4.9 stars)
  5. Conversion: Patient books consultation via practice site or calls directly

Both brands amplify the other. Doctor brand creates demand; practice brand converts it.

Content Cross-Promotion Strategy

  • Doctor Site (drjosephfield.com): Link to practice website in bio, footer, and key articles. Blog posts link to relevant service pages on practice site.
  • Practice Site (pccdsmiles.com): Embed YouTube videos from @dr_joseph_field. Link to doctor's credentials page. Include blog excerpts from drjosephfield.com.
  • Doctor Instagram (@dr_joseph_field): Bio link to drjosephfield.com. Stories link to practice website for booking. Captions include practice location.
  • Practice Instagram (@peninsulacosmeticdentistry): Cross-promote @dr_joseph_field in captions ("Watch Dr. Field's commentary on this case"). Stories link to doctor's YouTube channel.
  • YouTube: Dr. Field channel includes practice branding in channel header, video descriptions link to pccdsmiles.com and practice social accounts. Practice social embeds relevant YouTube videos.

Hero Case Content System

A repeatable, efficient system for turning one outstanding patient transformation into 8-12 pieces of multi-channel content. One hero case (monthly) yields: before/after post, carousel, doctor commentary video, blog article, patient testimonial clip, email content, and multiple repurposed assets.

Why This Matters

Premium cosmetic patients are high-consideration buyers. They research extensively, seek multiple forms of validation, and want to see outcomes at scale across different channels. One exceptional transformation documented comprehensively creates:

  • Awareness: YouTube video of doctor explaining clinical decisions reaches new prospects
  • Social proof: Before/after post and carousel on Instagram drives engagement and credibility
  • Trust: Patient testimonial video shows real emotional transformation
  • Authority: Blog article establishes doctor's approach and case selection philosophy
  • Education: Multiple pieces address objections and educate prospects on smile design process

Step-by-Step Hero Case Workflow

Step 1: Case Selection (Ongoing)

Monthly, identify one "hero case" that meets criteria:

  • Exceptional before/after transformation (dramatic improvement, difficult case successfully solved)
  • Patient willing to participate in content (testimonial, before/after photo usage, etc.)
  • Completed within last 3-6 months (fresh results, patient still engaged)
  • Fits monthly theme (April = awareness/transformation, May = trust/decision, June = premium positioning)
  • Aligns with top 3 priorities (veneers, implants, or smile makeover preferred)

Step 2: Patient Interview & Capture (Week 1 of content month)

Timing: Schedule 30-60 minutes with patient during follow-up appointment or dedicated content session.

Content Capture Checklist:

  • High-resolution before/after photos (at least 5-6 angles: full smile, close-up, 3/4 view, open smile, profile, lifestyle)
  • Patient testimonial video (1.5-2 minutes): Ask open-ended questions from UGC prompts (Section 7)
  • Patient story notes: Written answers to prompts ("What was holding you back?" "How do you feel now?" "What surprised you?")
  • Patient consent & usage rights signed (for photos, testimonial, name/initials in content)

Doctor Commentary Preparation:

  • Dr. Field reviews before/afters and patient file before recording
  • Identify 3-5 key clinical talking points: "Why this case was complex," "Our approach," "Design decisions," "Results and longevity"
  • Write 1-2 sentence summary of clinical narrative

Step 3: Doctor Commentary Video Recording (Week 1)

Format: 2-4 minute talking head video with before/after photos in background or displayed during video.

Recording Setup:

  • Professional background (clinical setting or branded backdrop)
  • Good lighting (natural or softbox)
  • High-quality audio (lavalier mic or external mic)
  • Phone or camera on tripod or handheld (stabilized)

Doctor Commentary Script Prompts:

"Today I want to show you a transformation we're really proud of. This patient came to us with [CLINICAL CHALLENGE]. Our approach was to [TREATMENT PLAN]. Here's what we achieved:"

"The beauty of [PROCEDURE/APPROACH] is that it's customizable to each patient's goals. This case is a great example of how personalization leads to exceptional outcomes."

Video Editing: Cut down to 2-4 minutes for YouTube; create shorter 30-60 second clips for Instagram Reels/TikTok.

UGC Capture Workflow

A systematized in-office process for capturing authentic user-generated content (patient testimonials, before/afters, candid videos) to fuel monthly content production and maintain consistent, authentic social proof.

Why UGC Matters

Branded content created by the practice is important for messaging control and professionalism. But authentic patient content—testimonials, candid videos, real before/afters—drives higher trust and engagement. Patients trust other patients more than they trust marketing messages. A systematized UGC capture workflow ensures consistent flow of authentic, high-quality patient content.

Team Roles & Responsibilities

Role Responsibility Frequency
Front Desk / Patient Coordinator Initial patient qualification (identify good candidates), send pre-appointment reminder/invitation, brief patients on content request, facilitate scheduling of content session Ongoing
Hygiene or Doctor (During Appointment) Ask patients informally about satisfaction and mention content opportunity, initiate conversation for verbal testimonial Every appointment (identify candidates)
Designated Content Coordinator (or Practice Manager) Schedule dedicated content sessions (15-30 min), conduct interviews using prompts, record video/audio, photograph before/afters, capture consent forms, organize files, upload to shared folder 2-3 times/week (4-6 UGC captures/month)
Doctor (Optional) Appear in some patient testimonial videos (brief intro/outro), provide commentary on cases for additional credibility layer Monthly (1-2 times/month)

Patient Identification & Qualification

Ideal UGC Candidates:

  • Completed cosmetic procedure (veneers, implants, smile makeover) 1-6 months ago
  • Exceptional satisfaction and visible confidence boost
  • Willing to be photographed/videotaped (verbal or written consent)
  • Can articulate their transformation and experience
  • Comfortable on camera (or willing to try)
  • Professional appearance/setting preference (not required, but can help)

Disqualify If:

  • Patient expresses discomfort with being filmed or named
  • Patient has ongoing concerns or complications
  • Patient lacks clarity on their transformation experience

Patient Recruitment Script

Timing: During appointment follow-up or when patient is visibly happy with results.

Script:

"We love how happy you look with your new smile! Our patients are our best marketing—would you be interested in sharing your story? It would just be a quick video (a few minutes) where you talk about your experience and how your smile has changed your confidence. We'd feature it on our Instagram and YouTube to help other people considering smile transformations. You'd be helping real people make the decision to transform their own smiles."

If Yes: "Great! We'll schedule a short 15-minute session with our team—super casual, just come in and chat about your experience. Can we get you on the calendar?"

If No or Unsure: "No problem! If you change your mind, just let us know. We always respect patient privacy first."

Content Capture Session Workflow

Duration: 15-30 minutes per session.

Setup:

  • Quiet space (office, conference room, or clinical area—background doesn't need to be perfect, but should be clean)
  • Good natural lighting (near window) or softbox/ring light
  • Phone or camera on tripod or handheld (stabilized)
  • Lavalier mic or external mic for audio quality (optional, but improves quality)
  • High-res camera for before/after photos

Content to Capture:

  1. High-resolution before/after photos (5 min): Multiple angles (full smile, close-up, 3/4 view, open smile, profile, candid/natural smile). Use phone camera on portrait mode or DSLR.
  2. Patient interview video (10-15 min): Record 1.5-2 minutes of patient answering open-ended questions from prompts below. Let them talk naturally; you'll edit down later.
  3. Optional: Doctor cameo (2-3 min): If patient is comfortable, have doctor join for final 1-2 minutes to congratulate patient or add brief commentary.
  4. Consent form (2 min): Patient signs consent allowing use of photos/video in marketing. Keep simple and clear.

UGC Interview Prompts (Patient Testimonials)

Use these prompts as conversation starters. Let patient talk naturally; don't read verbatim.

Confidence & Transformation
  • "Tell me about your smile before we did this work. What were you concerned about?"
  • "How has your confidence changed since your procedure?"
  • "What do you notice yourself doing differently now? (Smiling more, taking photos, speaking up in meetings?)"
  • "Has this impacted your personal or professional life?"
Experience & Process
  • "What was your expectation when you first came in? How did the process compare?"
  • "Tell me about your consultation experience. How did Dr. Field help you decide on your smile design?"
  • "How was the procedure itself? Any surprises?"
  • "What about recovery? Was it what you expected?"
Results & Satisfaction
  • "When you first saw the final result, what was your reaction?"
  • "How happy are you with the outcome?"
  • "If you had a friend considering this same procedure, what would you tell them?"
  • "Is this what you were hoping for?"
Lifestyle & Aspiration
  • "Tell me about a moment since the procedure when you were really happy with your smile."
  • "What does having a great smile mean to you?"
  • "Has this changed how you feel about yourself?"
Technical / Premium Positioning
  • "Did Dr. Field explain the approach and why it was right for you? How did that help you feel confident?"
  • "What did you think about the Smile Test Drive / Digital Smile Design?"
  • "How does your new smile look compared to what you imagined?"

Video Editing & Repurposing

From one 2-minute patient interview, create:

  • 30-second Instagram Reel clip: Extract most compelling quote or moment. Add captions, music, graphics.
  • 60-second Facebook/TikTok clip: Slightly longer version with intro text.
  • Longer 60-90 second YouTube testimonial: Full quote or two, more context, link to service page.
  • Multiple platform versions: Vertical (Instagram/TikTok), horizontal (YouTube), square (Facebook).

Tools: Use free editing software (CapCut, DaVinci Resolve, Canva Pro) or hire freelance editor ($50-200 per video).
