Executive Summary

Woodyard Periodontics is experiencing exceptional growth. Production surged 46.3% ($696K → $1.018M) driven by a dramatic 69% increase in new patient referrals (75 → 127) and strong operational improvements. This is a compelling growth story — the practice is successfully scaling its referral pipeline and capturing significantly more volume.

However, case acceptance and case value require attention. The critical finding: patients are accepting more treatment in general (DI case acceptance at 95.6%), but the specific perio treatments they were referred for are being accepted at a lower rate (60.8% from Referral Lab vs. 95.6% from DI). This suggests patients are cherry-picking smaller, less complex treatments rather than accepting full perio plans. Simultaneously, average case value declined $3,445 YoY. These two metrics together indicate an opportunity to improve case presentation and financial counseling.

Referral pipeline leakage is significant. Schedule-to-consult is 76.8% (below the 85% benchmark), and missed calls are alarmingly high at 57.1% — more than half of incoming calls are being missed. For a referral-based practice, missed calls from referring doctors or their patients directly mean lost referrals. This is the single biggest opportunity to recover revenue with minimal clinical or operational change.

The practice scorecard shows 14 of 19 metrics passing, with gaps primarily in the referral pipeline (call handling, schedule-to-consult) and case value metrics. Operational execution is strong — no-shows dropped to 0.9%, cancellations to 4%, and appointment confirmation jumped to 73.8%.

Key Insights & Perio-Specific Opportunities

Insight 1: Missed Calls are the Biggest Leak

The Issue: 57.1% of incoming calls are being missed. For a referral-based practice, each missed call is a lost referral — either the referring doctor calling with a case, or a referred patient calling to schedule. At 77 referrals/month, a 57% miss rate means ~44 referrals per month are not being answered.

The Opportunity: Implementing a call handling protocol (dedicated phone line during business hours, voicemail-to-text, automatic callback, or additional staff coverage) could recover significant volume with zero clinical change.

Projected Impact: If the practice captured just 50% of missed calls (22 additional referrals/month at $7,122 avg case value), the annual impact would be approximately $1.87M in additional production.

Insight 2: Schedule-to-Consult Leakage

The Issue: 76.8% of referred patients who are scheduled actually show up for their consult. This means ~23% of scheduled referrals are no-shows, cancellations, or reschedules that don't convert. This is a pre-clinical leakage point — the patient never gets to your chair.

Root Causes to Investigate:
  • Insurance verification delays (patient doesn't schedule until coverage is confirmed)
  • Long wait times to first consult (referred patient books slot but cancels before date)
  • Inconsistent confirmation protocol (appointment not confirmed, patient forgets)
  • Patient financial concerns (patient hesitates due to cost estimate)

Opportunity: Implement a referred-patient-specific confirmation protocol: confirm appointment 48 hours before, verify insurance 24 hours before, and call referring doctor to reinforce recommendation 72 hours before consult.

Projected Impact: Moving from 76.8% to 85% schedule-to-consult would convert an additional 6-7 referred patients per month. At 60.8% case acceptance and $7,122 per case, this yields approximately $310K additional annual production.

Insight 3: Case Acceptance Discrepancy (DI vs. Referral Lab)

The Issue: DI reports 95.6% case acceptance, but Referral Lab shows 60.8%. This 35-point gap means patients are accepting treatment, but not the full perio case they were referred for.

What's Happening: A referred patient accepts a limited scope (e.g., implant only) but declines the comprehensive plan (implant + bone graft + soft tissue graft). From DI's perspective, "treatment was accepted." From the referring doctor's perspective, the referral was only partially converted.

Why It Matters: You're missing 8-10% of case value per patient. With 127 new patients and an avg case value of $7,122, moving from 60.8% to 69% acceptance would recover approximately $90K per quarter, or $360K annually.

Opportunity:
  • Case Presentation Coaching: Train the doctor and clinical team on full-scope perio case presentation (implant + graft bundle, not implant OR graft)
  • Financial Counseling: Have a treatment coordinator discuss financing/payment plans BEFORE the clinical presentation, not after
  • Referring Doctor Communication: In the surgical report, specifically note which parts of the treatment plan were declined and why (patient request, insurance limitation, etc.). Referring doctors can reinforce the full plan at follow-up.

Insight 4: Case Value Declining

The Issue: Average case value dropped $3,445 year-over-year ($7,122 current vs. $10,567 prior). This is a significant headwind.

Possible Causes:
  • Case mix shift — more lower-value maintenance/extractions, fewer implant/graft cases
  • Patients choosing smaller scopes (see Insight 3)
  • Insurance pre-authorization limits forcing plan reductions
  • New referring doctors sending lower-complexity cases

Opportunity: Analyze top 20 referring doctors by case value. Identify which ones are sending lower-value cases and why. Then either (a) provide them with more high-complexity referral targets, or (b) schedule a case consultation to reorient them on complex case criteria.

Insight 5: Growth Masking Utilization Opportunity

The Opportunity: The practice grew 46% while VPWD actually declined. This suggests the practice is not at full capacity. Current schedule capacity could support 30+ VPWD, but only achieving 27.91. If the practice simply filled available chair time with referrals at the current conversion rate, additional production could exceed $150K per quarter.

Action: Conduct a weekly provider schedule audit. Look for gaps, late-start days, or cancelled blocks. Then target referral development toward filling those gaps.
