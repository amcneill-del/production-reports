Discovery & Revenue Growth Story

Weeks 1–2  |  [Date Range]

Before we recommend anything, we need to understand your practice. Phase 1 is about gathering the data that tells your Revenue Growth Story — how much capacity your doctors have, where your patients come from, and what types of care drive your production. This is the foundation everything else is built on.

⏱️ 1.1 — Doctor Capacity Analysis

▼

We start by understanding how much room your practice has to grow. Doctor capacity is measured as a percentage of available clinical hours per month that are currently scheduled. This tells us whether growth means filling open chair time or expanding hours.

What We're Measuring

  • Total available clinical hours per doctor per month
  • Currently scheduled hours per doctor per month
  • % of capacity utilized — the core metric in your Revenue Growth Story
  • Hygiene hours and recall rate as indicators of patient retention
TaskOwnerPriorityTarget
Pull doctor schedules — extract hours/month per provider from PMS Data High Week 1
Calculate % of doctor capacity utilized across all providers MM High Week 1
Identify open chair time windows and patterns (days of week, AM vs PM) MM Medium Week 1
Document hygiene capacity and recall compliance rate Data Medium Week 2

🔬 1.2 — Patient Funnel Breakdown

▼

We categorize your patient base into three tiers to understand where production comes from and where the growth opportunities live. This breakdown drives everything from marketing messaging to referral strategy.

The Three Funnel Categories

  • General Care — preventive, restorative, hygiene recall (the base of the funnel)
  • Advanced Care — implants, full-arch, complex restorative, ortho (high-value, high-production)
  • Specialty — perio, endo, oral surgery, pediatric (referral-driven or in-house specialty)

▼

This is the deliverable from Phase 1 — a concise narrative that ties together doctor capacity, patient funnel, and production data into a clear picture of where the practice is today and where the growth opportunity lives. This story becomes the foundation for every marketing decision that follows.

Phase 1 Exit Criteria

Digital Storefront Assessment

Weeks 3–5  |  [Date Range]

Your website and online presence are the first thing prospective patients see. Phase 2 is a thorough evaluation of your digital storefront — your website, Google Business Profile, and online reputation. We audit everything against 45+ criteria, score it, and determine the right path forward.

🌐 2.1 — Digital Storefront Audit

▼

We run your website through our comprehensive dental-website-audit — a 45+ criteria evaluation covering conversion strategy, UX, SEO, service lines, trust signals, NAP consistency, insurance/financing visibility, content quality, and technical performance. The result is an interactive scored dashboard.

Audit Categories

  • Conversion Strategy — CTAs, scheduling access, phone visibility, mobile experience
  • SEO & Discoverability — title tags, meta descriptions, schema markup, local SEO
  • Trust & Reputation — reviews integration, team bios, before/after galleries, credentials
  • Service Line Visibility — are your highest-value services easy to find and well-presented?
  • Technical Performance — page speed, mobile responsiveness, security, accessibility

🛤️ 2.2 — Website Path: Keep, Optimize, or Rebuild

▼

Based on audit results, we determine the right path for your website. Not every practice needs a new site — some need targeted fixes, others need a full rebuild. Here's how the decision works:

Website Decision Framework

Path A: Audit Score Passes — Optimize Current Site

If the current website scores well across our key criteria, we move to targeted optimizations — improving CTAs, adding missing service pages, enhancing trust signals, and tightening SEO. This is faster and preserves your existing domain authority.

Path B: Audit Score Fails — New Website from The Studio

If the website has fundamental issues (poor mobile experience, missing conversion paths, outdated design, broken UX), we recommend a new site designed and built by The Studio. We'll share a design concept, provide a build timeline, and coordinate the transition so there's no downtime.

Marketing Performance & Spend Analysis

Weeks 5–7  |  [Date Range]

Now that we understand your practice (Phase 1) and your digital presence (Phase 2), we look at your marketing investment. How much are you spending, where is it going, and what's it producing? The depth of this analysis depends on what data is available from your current or prior marketing vendors.

💰 3.1 — Marketing Spend Analysis

▼

How deep we can go depends on what data exists. If you're working with a digital marketing firm (or recently were), we can pull full performance data. If not, we work with what we have.

Data Availability Determines the Approach

Path A: Digital Firm Data Available — Full Marketing Spend Performance Audit

If we can access campaign data from your current or previous digital marketing firm (Google Ads, Meta Ads, call tracking, etc.), we run a full performance audit. This includes cost per lead, cost per new patient, channel-by-channel ROI, conversion rates at every stage of the funnel, and spend efficiency benchmarks. You'll see exactly what's working, what isn't, and where the waste is.

Path B: No Firm Data — Promotional Spend / New Patient Benchmarking

If detailed campaign data isn't available, we use your total promotional spend and new patient count to calculate a blended cost-per-new-patient. We then benchmark that against the rest of the Gen4 portfolio — showing where you fall relative to peer practices by market size, specialty mix, and maturity. This gives us a directional read even without granular data.
