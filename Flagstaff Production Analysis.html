Executive Summary

Flagstaff's production declined 7.7% year-over-year ($1,159,440 to $1,069,579), despite robust new patient growth (+41%) and improved case acceptance rates. The primary production drag is schedule utilization: Visits Per Working Day (VPWD) dropped significantly from 43.42 to 37.44, representing a -$149,511 impact—accounting for the majority of the decline. Appointment confirmation rates deteriorated sharply (59.4% to 45.1%), directly contributing to increased broken appointments and lower patient throughput. Positively, appointment leakage improved substantially (11.6% to 8.7%), and new patient case acceptance rose 3.2 points. Critical scheduling bottleneck: 3rd Next Available NP Appointment stands at 23 days versus the ≤7 day benchmark—a significant access constraint impacting new patient conversion. The opportunity lies in recovering schedule capacity, reducing the 23-day appointment gap to within 7 days, and rebuilding the appointment confirmation system to restore the patient flow baseline. The practice scorecard shows 6 of 11 scoreable metrics passing, with critical gaps in call conversion, scheduling access (23 days vs ≤7 benchmark), patient retention (NP re-appointment rate, cancellations), and online reputation (Yelp ratings). Unscheduled opportunity dollars decreased 17.6% YOY ($1,752,929 to $1,444,667), reflecting positive patient recovery momentum.
