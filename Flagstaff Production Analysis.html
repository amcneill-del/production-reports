Executive Summary

Flagstaff's production declined 7.7% year-over-year ($1,159,440 to $1,069,579), despite robust new patient growth (+41%) and improved case acceptance rates. The primary production drag is schedule utilization: Visits Per Working Day (VPWD) dropped significantly from 43.42 to 37.44, representing a -$149,511 impact—accounting for the majority of the decline. Appointment confirmation rates deteriorated sharply (59.4% to 45.1%), directly contributing to increased broken appointments and lower patient throughput. Positively, appointment leakage improved substantially (11.6% to 8.7%), and new patient case acceptance rose 3.2 points. The opportunity lies in recovering schedule capacity and rebuilding the appointment confirmation system to restore the patient flow baseline.

Top 3 Tactical Opportunities

1. Schedule Utilization Recovery (Priority: Urgent)

Current State: VPWD collapsed from 43.42 to 37.44 (down 6 visits/day), accounting for -$149,511 in lost production—the single largest drag on profitability. This is 166% of the total production decline.

Recommended Actions:

  • Capacity Audit: Verify provider schedule for Q1 2026: Were there unscheduled absences, reduced hours, or emergency closures? Compare provider availability hours to Q1 2025 baseline.
  • Supply-Side Verification: With 2,199 future appointments and 987 unscheduled patients ($1.44M opportunity), demand exists. Confirm that available provider time is being fully utilized for patient scheduling.
  • Hygiene Schedule Review: At 5.47 visits/hour, hygiene is not bottlenecked. Investigate whether restorative/provider schedules have open slots that could be filled with the existing patient pipeline.
  • Schedule Optimization: Implement daily huddles to review next-day schedule utilization and proactively fill open slots with unscheduled patients or short-notice appointments from the existing base.
  • Impact if Solved: Restoring VPWD to 43.42 would recover approximately $149,511 in production—nearly 100% of the year-over-year decline.

2. Appointment Confirmation Rebuild (Priority: High)

Current State: DI confirmed appointments crashed 45% (1,795 to 980) and confirmation rate dropped 14.3 points to 45.1%—the weakest confirmation metric. This directly feeds broken appointments (still 213 despite improvement from 310) and missing patient visits.

Recommended Actions:

  • DI System Audit: Verify the digital confirmation system is operational and that staff are actively soliciting confirmations at checkout. Check for system downtime or data sync issues during the reporting period.
  • Confirmation Protocol: Implement mandatory SMS/phone confirmations 48 hours pre-appointment. Create daily tracking dashboard showing confirmation %, target: 70%+.
  • Same-Day Confirmation: For appointments scheduled at checkout, ask patient to confirm on phone or via SMS before they leave the office to ensure commitment.
  • No-Show Root Cause: While no-show % improved (2% vs. 0.1%), broken appointments remain at 213 and cancellations at 210. Conduct brief exit interviews to understand cancellation drivers.
  • Impact if Solved: Restoring confirmation rate from 45.1% to 55% (historical baseline) would recapture approximately 80+ confirmed appointments and support VPWD recovery.

3. NP Hygiene Re-Appointment & Same-Day Recovery (Priority: High)

Current State: NP hygiene re-appointment % dropped sharply from 42.2% to 34.6%, a 7.6-point decline despite 41% more new patients. Same-day hygiene also declined 27% ($25K to $18K), representing lost production and broken continuity of care.

Recommended Actions:

  • NP Training Reinforcement: New patients must leave with a hygiene re-appointment scheduled before checkout. Make this a non-negotiable SOP: 100% of NPs leave with a scheduled hygiene visit.
  • Chairside Acceptance Scripts: Train clinicians to present same-day hygiene or restorative treatment at chair with clear rationale. Emphasize early intervention and patient benefits. Use case studies of successful same-day acceptance.
  • NP Hygiene Acceptance Campaign: Offer limited-time incentive for NPs to schedule hygiene re-appt at first visit (e.g., 10% off first cleaning, complimentary whitening consult). Track adherence daily.
  • Same-Day Supply Review: Verify that same-day treatment materials, time slots, and staff capacity support chairside treatment. Remove scheduling or supply barriers.
  • Impact if Solved: If NP hygiene re-appt improves from 34.6% to 42% (prior level), approximately 100+ additional hygiene appointments would be scheduled. Combined with same-day improvement, this could add $15-20K in annual production.
