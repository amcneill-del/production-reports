Pillar 2: Website & SEO Strategy

Current State: Your website scored 35% in the Digital Storefront Audit. Service Line Clarity is strong at 83% — your fishing, lodging, dining, and experiences pages are well-organized and compelling. However, SEO scored just 21%, Location/NAP is at 13% (your website shows a Colorado PO Box instead of your Ketchikan address), and Tracking is at 10% with no confirmed analytics. Most critically, no Google Business Profile was found — you're invisible on Google Maps.

Immediate (Week 1-2)

  1. Claim Google Business Profile for Ketchikan — This is priority #1. Add: Ketchikan lodge address, phone (907-931-9930), business hours, 5-10 lodge photos, description highlighting fishing expertise. This is the highest-leverage action you can take.
  2. Replace Colorado Springs PO Box with Ketchikan lodge address on every page (header, footer, contact page, About section). Update all citations and local directories.
  3. Add click-to-call tel: link to phone number (907-931-9930) in header and hero section. This drives direct calls and improves conversion rate.
  4. Rewrite title tag from "Home | Sportsman's Cove Lodge" to "Alaska Fishing Lodge | Ketchikan Guided Sportfishing | Sportsman's Cove" — includes primary keywords (Alaska, Ketchikan, fishing, lodge).
  5. Rewrite H1 from "Experience Alaska" to "World-Class Guided Fishing Lodge in Ketchikan, Alaska" — more specific, keyword-rich, benefit-focused.
  6. 301 redirect alaskasbestlodge.com to sportsmanscovelodge.com — consolidates dual domains, prevents SEO fragmentation, and clarifies your primary brand domain.
  7. Fix "ACCOMODATIONS" spelling error on the lodging page (correct: "ACCOMMODATIONS").

Short-Term (Month 1)

  1. Add LocalBusiness schema markup (JSON-LD) to homepage with complete business data: name, address, phone, website, image, description, coordinates. This improves rich snippet appearance in search results.
  2. Write captain bios with detailed credentials: years of experience, fishing specialties, certifications, personal stories. Position each guide as a trusted expert. Add photos.
  3. Create FAQ page covering: What to bring? How do I ship my catch? What's the weather like? Best seasons? Cancellation policy? Fishing licenses? Dietary restrictions? Payment terms?
  4. Install GA4 and set up conversion tracking for: Book Now button clicks, form submissions, phone clicks. Set up goals for newsletter signups.
  5. Add privacy policy and terms of service to footer. Links to these build trust and address audit gaps.
  6. Label all logo badges on homepage with organization names (e.g., "Conservation Commission of Alaska", "Better Business Bureau", etc.). This improves trust-building score.
  7. Submit XML sitemap to Google Search Console and request indexing.

Medium-Term (Month 2-3)

  1. Create seasonal landing pages (King Salmon season, Halibut season) with season-specific copy, photos, catch data, and CTAs.
  2. Launch a blog with monthly fishing reports and guest stories. Start with 2-3 posts to establish content freshness signals.
  3. Build a "Trophy Catches" gallery organized by species (King Salmon, Halibut, Silver Salmon, Rockfish) using TripAdvisor guest photos.
  4. Add boat fleet specifications and tackle details to build trust and answer common guest questions.
  5. Consider migrating off Wix for better SEO control and customization (long-term optimization). Current platform has limitations on schema, internal linking, and analytics.

Key Metrics to Track: Organic traffic, Google Maps visibility (search rankings for "Alaska fishing lodge"), keyword rankings for "Ketchikan fishing", "guided fishing Alaska", Book Now click rate, form submission rate, bounce rate, pages per session.

Pillar 3: Review Strategy

Current State: TripAdvisor is your strongest review platform with 23 reviews, 68 guest photos, and a 4.5-star rating. Yelp has 29 photos and strong engagement. However, no Google Business Profile was found — meaning you have ZERO Google reviews. For a lodge that depends on "Alaska fishing lodge" searches, this is the most critical gap. Your Instagram is strong (749 followers, 303 posts), but Facebook is not indexed by Google.

Immediate (Week 1-2)

  1. Claim Google Business Profile (same action as SEO pillar). This enables Google reviews, the most trusted review source for local businesses.
  2. Respond to every existing TripAdvisor review — thank positive reviewers, address any concerns professionally, and show you care. This improves review credibility.
  3. Create a post-trip review request email template targeting Google first: "Help other Alaska fishing enthusiasts find us — leave a review on Google." Follow with TripAdvisor request in separate email. Include direct links to review pages.
  4. Implement SMS review request to departing guests (Day 2 after checkout). Higher response rate than email alone.

Short-Term (Month 1)

  1. Target 20+ Google reviews within 60 days through email + SMS campaigns to your last 50 guests.
  2. Monitor review sentiment — tag positive reviews by theme (fishing success, crew service, meals, scenery) to identify strengths.
  3. Create a "Reviews" page on website aggregating top quotes from Google, TripAdvisor, and Yelp.
  4. Encourage photo uploads alongside reviews. Visual reviews (with photos) have 4x higher conversion impact.

Key Metrics: Google review count and rating, TripAdvisor review velocity, Yelp review count, average star rating across platforms, photo uploads per review, response rate to review requests.
