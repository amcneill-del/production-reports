Executive Summary

Rossville Family Dental delivered strong production growth of +$124,759 (+29.2%) year-over-year in Q1 2026, reaching $551,826 in gross production. The practice scorecard shows 12 of 19 metrics passing, with key gaps in patient acquisition infrastructure (call conversion, missed calls, scheduling access) and online reputation depth (Yelp presence, Google review velocity). New patient volume held steady at 129 (43/month), well above the 30/month benchmark, and the team achieved notable improvements in hygiene re-appointment (93%, up from 88.2%), NP hygiene re-appointment (59.7%, up from 55.2%), and appointment throughput (+334 completed appointments YoY). The primary opportunities for continued growth center on reducing the 37% missed call rate, improving the 12-day wait for a new patient appointment, and building Yelp review volume to strengthen the practice's local search visibility.

Action Plan — Organized by Support Team

Recommendations are prioritized based on scorecard failures and the largest production recovery opportunities. Each action specifies an owner, workflow, and estimated impact.

Marketing

Accelerate Google Review Velocity

Current pace is 5 reviews/month vs. the 10/month benchmark. With a 99 sentiment score, patient satisfaction is high — the gap is in systematic ask workflows. Implement post-visit review request via Swell/text automation within 2 hours of checkout. Target: 10+ Google reviews per month.

Owner: Marketing  |  DI Workflow: N/A (Swell or manual)  |  Impact: Improved local SEO ranking and trust signals

Yelp Profile Remediation

With only 5 Yelp reviews at 3.4 stars, a single positive or negative review swings the rating significantly. Claim/optimize the Yelp Business Page. Do not directly solicit Yelp reviews (violates Yelp TOS), but ensure the Yelp profile is complete with photos, services, and updated hours. Consider Yelp Ads check-in offers to encourage organic reviews.

Owner: Marketing  |  Impact: Local search credibility, especially for patients who cross-reference platforms

Patient Reactivation Campaign — 6+ Month Overdue

With 213 unscheduled patients in the 0-6 month window (up from 181), manual outreach can recapture production from patients who are drifting. Pull the 6+ month overdue patient list from Dental Intel Analytics (not Engagement — inactive patients are excluded from Engagement). Assign manual phone outreach to front desk staff.

Owner: Marketing + Office Manager  |  DI Workflow: Analytics → Pull overdue list → Export for manual calls  |  Impact: At $896 annual patient value, recapturing 20 patients = ~$17,920

Operations

Reduce 3rd Next Available from 12 Days to ≤7 Days

A 12-day wait for a new patient appointment creates friction and increases the chance of losing prospective patients to competitors. Conduct a weekly provider schedule audit to identify unused chair time. Consider adding dedicated NP slots in morning blocks. If demand consistently exceeds capacity, evaluate adding a provider day or expanding hours.

Owner: Operations / Office Manager  |  DI Workflow: N/A (scheduling system review)  |  Impact: Faster access → higher NP conversion from phone inquiries

48-Hour Appointment Confirmation Protocol

DI confirmation rate is 54.3% — below the 60% benchmark. Set up a confirmation TouchPoint in Dental Intel Engagement triggered 48 hours before appointment, with a day-of follow-up for unconfirmed. For appointments still unconfirmed within 24 hours, manual phone confirmation by front desk. Use the Park Column to fill any gaps from unconfirmed patients.

Owner: Office Manager  |  DI Workflow: Engagement → TouchPoint (48hr pre-appt) + manual phone backup  |  Impact: Reducing unconfirmed appointments protects scheduled production and reduces same-day gaps

Address Rising Cancellation Rate

Cancellations rose from 3.0% (43) to 4.5% (85) — still within benchmark but trending in the wrong direction with nearly double the volume. Review cancellation reasons, implement 48-hour cancellation policy communication, and maintain a short-notice fill list to replace cancelled appointments same-day.

Owner: Operations / Office Manager  |  Impact: Each percentage point of cancellation at current volume = ~$5,500 in at-risk production

Clinical

Case Acceptance Dollar Improvement Strategy

Case $ Accepted % declined from 63.5% to 40.3% as the practice presented significantly more comprehensive treatment ($782K vs. $415K). The team is diagnosing well — the opportunity is in helping patients accept larger treatment plans. Standardize treatment plan presentation with visual aids, phase treatment into manageable segments, and connect patients with financing options before presenting the full plan.

Owner: Clinical + RCM  |  Impact: Each 5pp improvement in Case $ Accepted % = ~$39,128 in additional accepted treatment

Hygiene-to-Restorative Handoff Improvement

The handoff ratio of 0.71 (below 0.85 benchmark) means roughly 29% of hygiene patients are not converting to restorative visits. Focus on hygienist-to-doctor warm handoffs with specific findings communicated chairside. Ensure every hygiene visit includes a clinical exam with documented treatment recommendations.

Owner: Clinical  |  Impact: Improving to 0.85 = ~122 additional restorative visits × $479/exam = ~$58,438 potential

Sustain Same-Day Restorative Momentum

Same-day restorative treatment grew +$55K (+37.6%) and Case Diagnosed % Restorative jumped to 46.5%. This is a clinical strength to protect. Continue empowering the clinical team to identify and present chairside treatment. Monitor hygiene same-day $ (declined 32%) to ensure hygienists also maintain chairside diagnosis habits.

Owner: Clinical  |  Impact: Maintaining trajectory protects $202K+ in quarterly same-day restorative production

Office Manager

NP Follow-Up Protocol — 2nd Appointment Within 24 Hours

NP Hygiene Re-Appointment improved to 59.7% (from 55.2%) but is still below the 75%+ target for retention. Before each new patient leaves, confirm their second appointment is booked. Office Manager reviews prior day's NP list each morning against scheduled appointments. Any NP without a second appointment gets a phone call within 24 hours. This is a manual process — Dental Intel Engagement cannot automate individual follow-up task creation.

Owner: Office Manager  |  DI Workflow: N/A (manual checklist)  |  Impact: Each 10pp improvement in NP re-appt captures ~13 additional returning patients per quarter

Referral Source Entry Compliance Audit

Missing referral source jumped from 0.8% to 17.8% (23 of 129 NPs). Implement a mandatory referral source field at patient intake. Office Manager conducts daily audit of new patient records — any missing referral source must be completed by end of business day. Without accurate referral data, it becomes impossible to evaluate which marketing channels are working.

Owner: Office Manager  |  DI Workflow: Analytics → NP list → daily compliance check  |  Impact: Accurate attribution protects marketing budget allocation decisions

RCM (Revenue Cycle Management)

Payment Plan Architecture Review

With Case $ Presented nearly doubling but acceptance % declining, patients may be hesitant about the financial commitment of larger treatment plans. Review and expand financing options (CareCredit, Sunbit, in-house payment plans). Ensure treatment coordinators present payment options during — not after — the treatment plan conversation.

Owner: RCM + Finance  |  Impact: Directly supports Case $ Accepted % improvement — each 5pp = ~$39K additional revenue

Doctor

Schedule Availability & Capacity Review

With a 12-day wait for new patient appointments and VPWD declining (27.13 → 24.66), there may be an opportunity to realign provider schedule templates with patient demand patterns. Review weekly schedule utilization and consider reallocating blocks to maximize NP access while maintaining production per visit.

Owner: Doctor + Operations  |  Impact: Reducing wait time from 12 to ≤7 days directly supports new patient acquisition and call conversion
