Critical Issues & Recommendations

Priority-ranked findings with actionable recommendations to drive marketing performance and revenue recovery.

CRITICAL ISSUES (Revenue Impact)

Critical #1
Case Acceptance Rate Declining (-4.3pp)
More treatment is being presented ($3.15M vs $2.68M in case value) but acceptance rate dropped from 34.6% to 30.3%. This represents approximately $135,000 in additional lost revenue opportunity.

Investigate:
  • Are pricing conversations happening at the right time?
  • Is the team presenting financing options to patients?
  • Are treatment coordinators adequately trained in case presentation?
  • What objections are coming up most frequently?
  • Compare case acceptance by provider — identify top performers and replicate their approach
Critical #2
Patient Net Growth Collapsed (-62.5%)
Net growth dropped from 267 to 100 patients. Despite similar new patient numbers (627 vs 645), patient attrition is significantly higher. Combined with rising cancellations, this signals a retention crisis.

Action Items:
  • Analyze patient churn data by cohort — when do patients drop off?
  • Review patient experience feedback and exit surveys
  • Conduct "lost patient" outreach to understand why they stopped coming
  • Implement patient retention program with early warning triggers
  • Improve onboarding and first 6-month experience for new patients
Critical #3
NP Call Conversion FAILING at ALL 3 Locations
All three locations are below the 55% benchmark: Eagle Mountain (42%), Saratoga Springs (45%), Daybreak (43%). Every unconverted call is a lost new patient. At approximately $700 annual patient value, fixing this could add $200,000+ annually to gross production.

Recommended Solutions:
  • Implement call recording and quality assurance program — identify successful scripts
  • Provide front-desk staff training in phone communication and objection handling
  • Mystery shop call performance to benchmark against top performers in the industry
  • Create incentive structure tied to conversion rate improvement
  • Ensure new patient scheduling has clear availability — reduce "no appointments available" responses
Critical #4
Cancellation Rate Spiked (+5.4pp)
Cancellation rate jumped from 2.3% to 7.7% overall. Saratoga Springs is worst at 10.5% (was 0.4% in 2025). This indicates either reduced patient engagement or operational issues that need immediate investigation.

Investigation Required:
  • Identify reason codes for cancellations — see if there's a pattern
  • Check if cancellations are concentrated among specific appointment types or providers
  • Review reminder/confirmation process — improve messaging and engagement
  • Implement cancellation prevention workflow with automated rebooking for Saratoga Springs
  • Track what changed between 2025 and 2026 — staffing, scheduling system, policies?

HIGH PRIORITY ISSUES

High Priority #5
Google Review Velocity Below Benchmark
Eagle Mountain (2/month) and Daybreak (3/month) are far below the 10/month benchmark. Only Saratoga Springs (16/month) is passing. Consistent review generation is critical for SEO rankings and patient acquisition.

Solution: Implement systematic review request workflow through Swell. Automate post-appointment review requests via text/email with direct Google review links.
High Priority #6
Saratoga Springs Missed Call Rate (27.8%)
Nearly 1 in 3 calls missed. This is the worst-performing metric across all locations and directly impacts new patient acquisition. These are lost revenue opportunities.

Immediate Actions:
  • Audit current phone staffing levels during peak call hours
  • Consider additional front desk staff or answering service for peak times
  • Implement call routing or queuing system to better manage call volume
  • Review current phone system for technical issues
High Priority #7
Billing Accuracy Issues Undermining Trust
Swell sentiment analysis and Yelp reviews both flag billing as the top complaint (especially Saratoga Springs & Eagle Mountain). This destroys patient trust, drives negative reviews, and likely contributes to patient attrition.

Recommended Audit:
  • Conduct billing accuracy review — identify systemic errors
  • Review billing staff training and processes
  • Implement quality assurance checks on all patient statements
  • Proactively contact patients with billing errors to resolve quickly
  • Track billing-related cancellations and no-shows
High Priority #8
Yelp Ratings Damaging Brand Reputation
Eagle Mountain 2.8 stars is significantly damaging. Saratoga has zero Yelp reviews. Daybreak 3.6 is below acceptable. Active reputation management and review generation is needed immediately.

Strategy: Implement targeted Yelp review request campaigns. Monitor Yelp closely for negative reviews and respond professionally within 24 hours.

MODERATE PRIORITY ITEMS

Moderate #9
Unscheduled Active Patients Growing ($2.7M Lost Opportunity)
4,015 patients with treatment needs but no scheduled appointments. This represents significant lost revenue opportunity. Reactivation campaigns could recover substantial production.

Recommendation: Launch patient reactivation program targeting unscheduled actives with special offers, flexible scheduling, and personalized outreach.
